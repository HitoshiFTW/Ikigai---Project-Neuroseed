## 3. Distributed Multi-Column Cortex

The singular cortical column was replaced by a `MultiColumnCortex` architectural container consisting of three specialized functional domains: `Visual`, `Auditory`, and `Somatic`. Every column maintains a precise 3-layer architecture:
- Layer 4 (sensory receipt)
- Layer 2/3 (associative integration & recurrence)
- Layer 5 (motor projection)
- Local inhibitory interneurons for winner-take-all (WTA) stability

### 3.1 Neural Expansion & Safety
The total organism population was expanded to approximately 250 explicitly modeled neurons. To allow cross-modal association without explosive instability, lateral excitatory pathways were drawn across the columns (e.g., `Visual L2/3` → `Auditory L2/3`). Crucially, we limited structural connectivity to an extreme sparse factor (5% - 8%) and instantiated a rigid **1-tick synaptic delay** to forcefully decouple the oscillatory cascade, preventing immediate system-wide synchronization.

## 4. Predictive Processing via Localized Surprise

A mathematically strict formulation of Karl Friston’s predictive coding doctrine @[Digital Organism Neuroscience Research Synthesis.txt] was integrated successfully. L2/3 populations now actively generate top-down generative predictions attempting to guess the incoming sensory state of L4.

We measure empirical scalar error as:
`error = actual_L4_state - predicted_L4_state`

Crucially, in Day 13 we transitioned the meaning of "error" from computational loss to actual neural voltage. Prediction error is explicitly traced physically backward through the specific elements of the prediction weight matrix. Only the specific L2/3 neurons directly responsible for pushing an inaccurate prediction receive physical voltage excitation. 

This localized physical **Surprise Burst** is scaled by ambient Norepinephrine levels:
`surprise_gain = error × (1 + norepinephrine)`

This physiologically mirrors cortical gain modulation during novel or alarming stimuli, allowing the network to become genuinely "surprise-sensitive" while maintaining baseline stability.

## 5. Basal Ganglia Action Selection

Motor outputs are no longer directly mapped to Python behaviors. Cortical L5 output populations now act as generic input drives for an enclosed `BasalGangliaSystem` executing action selection via an inhibitory sequence.

The system was fitted with three innate biological action channels: `Approach`, `Withdraw`, and `Explore`.

### 5.1 The Gating Sequence
1. Total L5 spiking output pushes parallel drive signals into the simulated **Striatum**.
2. A competition occurs for the highest concurrent channel drive.
3. The Globus Pallidus internus (**GPi**) sits in a constant state of tonic inhibition (closing the Thalamic gate).
4. Only when a single Striatal drive overwhelms the required threshold (≈1.5) does the GPi relax.
5. With the GPi inhibited, the **Thalamus** is disinhibited, enabling the action channel to cross the physiological threshold and cleanly execute as recognized behavior.

This fundamentally introduces organic, behaviorally realistic competition where conflicting actions (i.e. approaching and withdrawing simultaneously) suppress each other neutrally rather than crashing the system state.
