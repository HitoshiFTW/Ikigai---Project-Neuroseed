## 1. Objective

Day 35 addresses the absence of a learning-relevant signal. At the end of Day 34, the organism exhibits behavioral diversity (entropy 0.6220 bits, three action types), but the adjusted_credit_signal (ACS) is effectively constant across all waking ticks. The diagnostic run over 5000 ticks with all Day 33 and Day 34 flags enabled reveals ACS std = 0.0036, temporal_depth saturated at 10 for 99.8% of ticks (permanently above BG_DEPTH_WAIT), and 100% edit_code via fallback_bg wait monopoly. The TemporalCreditBridge BUFFER_MAXLEN = 10 fills after 10 waking ticks; depth_factor = min(depth/10, 1.0) = 1.0 on every subsequent tick; the depth axis of the credit computation is permanently frozen. The actual_reward signal is near-constant because the BG path always produces wait (depth >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT always triggers), and wait carries no action-dependent outcome differentiation. The objective is to restore differentiation to the reward pathway, push the confidence distribution into the decision boundary where fallback routing activates, and break the depth-based policy monopoly that collapses behavioral diversity under temporal integration -- all without modifying reward formulas, routing constants, or prior validated structure, and with zero behavioral drift when new flags are disabled.

## 2. Implemented Changes

**Pack 1 -- Action-State Coupling Layer (REVISED)**

Introduces action-dependent bias into `energy_coupling_bridge.energy_delta`, establishing a deterministic causal path from the executed action type to the outcome credit signal. The bias table (frozen D35P1-R1): explore +0.010, approach +0.005, edit_code +0.003, idle_recover -0.008; all other action types 0.0. The bias is applied after `energy_coupling_bridge.update()` has written energy_delta and before `cognitive_metabolic_regulator.update()` reads it. The update is clamped to max(0.0, energy_coupling_bridge.energy_delta + bias) to prevent negative energy delta. The causal chain is: action_type -> energy_delta -> energy_gain (= 1 - energy_delta / 0.12) -> actual_reward (energy_gain * 0.40 + goal_progress * 0.30 + stability * 0.20 - fatigue * 0.10) -> temporal_credit -> adjusted_credit_signal.

The initial implementation targeted `l23.energy` and `pp.error` directly. These do not propagate to actual_reward: energy_gain is computed from `energy_coupling_bridge.energy_delta` (the bridge's internal reasoning cost), not from raw l23.energy; and pp.error is not an input to the OutcomeEvaluator formula. Both variables are surface state. The correct lever is the bridge's energy_delta field. Pack 1 is guarded by `enable_action_state_coupling` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The necessity: without Pack 1, every action type produces the same actual_reward on every tick. OutcomeEvaluator reads energy_delta from the bridge (dominated by reasoning cost, which is identical across action types), goal_progress from binding_confidence (fixed by path), and stability from a smoothed history. None of these differentiate action types. ACS is informationally decoupled from the organism's behavioral output.

**Pack 2 -- Confidence Compression (Near-Threshold Regime)**

Introduces state-aware compression of `top_confidence` inside ActionConfidenceEvaluator, applied after the existing Pack 1 dampening block. Compression activates when the system is overly stable: `enable_confidence_compression` is True, `top_confidence > 0.55`, and `pp.error < 0.05` (low prediction error = high stability). The compression magnitude is `0.05 * (1.0 - pe / 0.05)`, scaled linearly from full compression (0.05) at pe = 0.00 to zero compression at pe = 0.05. The floor constraint is `max(0.55, top_confidence - compression)`. The constant MAX_COMPRESSION = 0.05 is implicit in the formula. Pack 2 is guarded by `enable_confidence_compression` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The floor constraint is critical. The initial implementation used `max(0.0, top_confidence - compression)`. With baseline top_confidence at approximately 0.622 and MAX_COMPRESSION = 0.05, unconstrained subtraction brings top_confidence to approximately 0.503. At 0.503, the routing correction (Day 34 Pack 3) sends all ticks to fallback_bg. In fallback_bg, `binding_confidence = 0.0` permanently (by ABG implementation: fallback_bg sets `self.binding_confidence = 0.0`). Since goal_progress = binding_confidence in the OutcomeEvaluator, goal_progress drops to zero on all ticks. actual_reward variance collapses. ACS std decreased from 0.00372 to 0.00261 with the unconstrained floor -- the opposite of the target. The floor at 0.55 keeps top_confidence in the fallback_reasoning zone [0.55, 0.60), where binding_confidence is non-zero (set to the clamped top_conf) and varies tick-to-tick with pe, producing variable goal_progress, variable actual_reward, and a 2.5x ACS std increase.

**Pack 3 -- BG Policy Decomposition**

Breaks the depth-based action monopoly inside ActionBindingGate. In the Day 34 BG policy, the priority order is: energy < BG_ENERGY_LOW -> idle_recover; depth >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT -> wait; top_conf < BG_TC_EXPLORE -> explore; top_conf < BG_TC_APPROACH -> approach; else idle_recover. With all Day 33 temporal packs enabled, temporal_depth saturates at 10 (TemporalCreditBridge BUFFER_MAXLEN = 10) within the first 10 waking ticks and remains at 10 permanently. Because 10 >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT (5), the depth branch fires before any confidence-based branch on every tick where energy >= BG_ENERGY_LOW (approximately 87% of BG ticks). The result: wait dominates within fallback_bg (59.3% of BG ticks; the remaining 40.7% go to idle_recover via the energy check, which fires before depth). No approach or explore action is produced under any state.

Pack 3 decomposes the `depth >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT` branch from a fixed assignment into a sub-policy. When `enable_bg_decomposition` is True, entry into the depth branch applies: if `_IKG.bg_pe > 0.05` -> explore; elif `top_conf < BG_TC_APPROACH` -> approach; else -> wait. When disabled, behavior is `bg_name = 'wait'` identically to the prior implementation. Depth still gates entry into this branch; Pack 3 changes only what happens inside it. The `_IKG.bg_pe` field is populated per tick by injecting `_IKG.bg_pe = float(pp.error)` after `pred_err = pp.update(signal)` in the experiment harness, parallel to the existing bg_energy injection established in Day 34 Pack 4. Pack 3 is guarded by `enable_bg_decomposition` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

## 3. System Behavior (Post-Change)

With all Day 33, Day 34, and Day 35 flags enabled over 5000 waking ticks, the system operates as follows. Top_confidence is compressed from its baseline of approximately 0.622 to approximately 0.504 by the combined effect of Pack 1 dampening (Day 34 Pack 1, which applies calibration_delta-based dampening) and Pack 2 compression (which applies pe-based compression when tc > 0.55 and pe < 0.05). At 0.504, all ticks fall below REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW (0.55) and route to fallback_bg via the Day 34 Pack 3 routing correction.

Within the fallback_bg regime, the Day 34 Pack 4 priority order applies. Energy < BG_ENERGY_LOW (0.30) accounts for approximately 9% of waking ticks, routing to idle_recover. On the remaining 91%, depth = 10 >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT (5) enters the decomposed branch. Inside that branch: `_IKG.bg_pe` (pp.error, injected from the live prediction error signal) exceeds 0.05 on approximately 1.6% of total waking ticks, routing to explore. On the majority of ticks, pp.error < 0.05 and top_conf = 0.504 < BG_TC_APPROACH (0.53), routing to approach. A single tick in 5000 meets the wait condition (top_conf >= 0.53 with low PE). The bound path (top_conf >= BINDING_THRESHOLD = 0.60) is not reached under active Pack 2 compression.

The Pack 1 action-state coupling introduces differentiated energy_delta values across this distribution: approach (bias +0.005) produces higher energy cost and lower energy_gain than idle_recover (bias -0.008, which reduces cost). This creates a non-trivial actual_reward distribution across action types, which propagates through temporal_credit to ACS.

## 5. Key Findings

- **The reward pathway depends on energy_delta in the bridge, not on raw energy state.** The OutcomeEvaluator formula `energy_gain = 1 - energy_delta / 0.12` reads from `energy_coupling_bridge.energy_delta`, a field representing the reasoning cost computed internally by the bridge during its `update()` call. Modifying `l23.energy` or `pp.error` directly has no effect on energy_gain. The causal path from behavioral choice to credit requires targeting the bridge's cost output, not the organism's surface metabolic state. This is the structural reason the initial Pack 1 implementation had zero effect on ACS despite correctly implementing the intended action differentiation logic.

- **Confidence dynamics produce attractor states that are not visible until the full pipeline runs.** Pack 2 without the floor constraint tipped the system from the high-confidence attractor (tc ~0.622, bound path dominant) into the fallback_bg low-confidence attractor (tc ~0.503, fallback_bg dominant). In the low-confidence attractor, binding_confidence = 0.0 on every tick, goal_progress = 0.0 on every tick, and actual_reward variance collapses. The attractor is a stable fixed point: once tc drops below REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW (0.55), routing to fallback_bg sets binding_confidence to zero, which removes the goal_progress term from actual_reward, which makes reward near-constant, which creates no credit gradient to perturb the system. The floor at 0.55 prevents entry into this attractor by maintaining tc in the fallback_reasoning zone where binding_confidence tracks top_conf.

- **Temporal depth saturation creates a permanent policy monopoly under temporal integration.** TemporalCreditBridge BUFFER_MAXLEN = 10 causes temporal_depth to saturate at 10 after 10 waking ticks. Because BG_DEPTH_WAIT = 5 and 10 >= 5 on every subsequent tick, the depth branch of the BG policy fires before any confidence-based branch for the remainder of the run (on all ticks where energy >= BG_ENERGY_LOW). The Day 34 BG policy design -- which intended depth to be one of three orthogonal axes -- becomes a two-branch policy in practice: energy (firing 9-23% of ticks) vs depth (firing the rest). The depth assignment was unconditional: `bg_name = 'wait'`. Under temporal integration, this produces wait monopoly inside the depth branch on 100% of entries.

- **Multi-signal decomposition within a depth-gated branch restores diversity without modifying the gating condition.** Pack 3 leaves the `depth >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT` entry condition unchanged. Inside that branch, PE and tc provide two independent signals with real variance: pp.error ranges from near-zero to above 0.05, and top_conf sits at approximately 0.504 (below BG_TC_APPROACH = 0.53) under Pack 2 compression. The result is approach-dominated with occasional explore (PE > 0.05), which is behaviorally richer than unconditional wait. The monopoly is broken by reusing signals already present in the system rather than by introducing new state.

- **Learning signal structure requires the full causal chain to be functional simultaneously.** ACS std at Day 34 baseline is 0.0036 despite routing being active. The reason: temporal_credit only updates when actual_reward != 0, actual_reward is near-constant because action types are not credit-differentiated (edit_code 100% monoculture), and depth_factor = 1.0 permanently (depth saturated). Pack 1 alone increases credit variance if action distribution is diverse; Pack 2 alone increases credit variance if the routing activates the fallback_reasoning zone; Pack 3 alone increases credit variance if Pack 1 has made action types credit-distinguishable. None of the three packs is effective in isolation. The 4.4x ACS std increase requires all three operating together.

- **Credit is now monotonically ordered by metabolic cost.** With Pack 1 active, the ACS mean rank is: edit_code > idle_recover > approach > explore. The ordering matches the action-type energy_delta bias (idle_recover most negative = cheapest; explore most positive = most expensive) with the exception of edit_code, which earns highest credit via the bound path's high binding_confidence (goal_progress contribution). This is a biologically coherent signal: low-cost actions that stabilize the organism earn more credit than high-cost exploration.

## 6. Failure Modes / Constraints

**Pack 1 initial implementation targeted the wrong layer.** The first version injected `l23.energy -= delta` and `pp.error += delta` immediately before the Day 29 metabolic cost block. These modifications change surface state but not the bridge's internal cost field. `energy_coupling_bridge.energy_delta` is set by the bridge's `update()` method based on the reasoning cost model, which does not read from l23.energy directly. The fix required identifying the exact read point (`energy_gain = 1 - energy_coupling_bridge.energy_delta / 0.12` inside OutcomeEvaluator) and tracing backward to find the writable field (`energy_coupling_bridge.energy_delta` after bridge.update() has set it). Any injection before `energy_coupling_bridge.update()` would be overwritten; injection at the correct point (between bridge.update() and cognitive_metabolic_regulator.update()) persists to OutcomeEvaluator.

**Pack 2 attractor failure.** Detailed above in Key Findings. The critical observation is that the failure mode is not visible from internal ACE diagnostics alone: top_confidence correctly decreases, compression correctly fires, tc std correctly increases. The failure emerges only when the full pipeline runs and the interaction between fallback_bg path selection and binding_confidence = 0.0 collapses the downstream reward signal. The fix (floor at 0.55) was identified by tracing the exact binding_confidence assignment in ActionBindingGate: `self.binding_confidence = 0.0` unconditionally on the fallback_bg path, while `self.binding_confidence = self._clamp(top_conf)` on the fallback_reasoning path.

**Pack 3 does not achieve the target distribution shape.** The specification described a target spread of wait 30-60%, approach 20-40%, explore 10-30%. The actual distribution (approach 88.6%, idle_recover 9.1%, explore 1.6%, wait ~0%) is approach-dominated because top_conf = 0.504 < BG_TC_APPROACH = 0.53 on essentially every tick where PE <= 0.05 (approximately 98.4% of depth-branch entries). The wait branch (top_conf >= 0.53) is unreachable at the current compression level. Explore (PE > 0.05) fires only 1.6% of the time. The distribution is determined by the actual system state (compressed tc at 0.504), not by the policy thresholds as designed for a more variable tc. The monopoly is broken (from 100% wait to 0% wait within the depth branch), but the replacement is approach-dominated rather than balanced. This is not a correctness failure -- the policy is operating correctly given the inputs -- but the intended three-way distribution requires either higher tc variance or threshold adjustment.

## 7. Architectural Insights

**The causal structure of the reward pathway must be traced from the learnable signal backward, not from the action forward.** Intervening at the wrong point in a causal chain produces changes that are invisible to the objective. l23.energy, pp.error, and binding_confidence all appear in the organism's state and are causally related to learning; none of them is the correct intervention point for action-to-credit coupling. The correct point is the unique variable that OutcomeEvaluator reads and that is not overwritten between the desired intervention and the read. This is engineering discipline, not biological fidelity: tracing the dataflow from the target variable backward to the nearest writable field.

**Confidence thresholds create non-linear regime boundaries.** The three routing regimes (bound, fallback_reasoning, fallback_bg) are separated by hard thresholds (BINDING_THRESHOLD = 0.60, REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW = 0.55). Small changes in top_confidence near these thresholds produce large changes in downstream pipeline behavior: binding_confidence flips between a tracked value and zero, goal_progress jumps between 0.55 and 0.00, actual_reward changes by approximately 0.165 (= 0.55 * 0.30). Pack 2's floor constraint is not a soft mitigation -- it is a regime boundary constraint that prevents the system from crossing from the fallback_reasoning regime into the fallback_bg regime. Managing attractor states in systems with hard thresholds requires identifying the threshold crossings and ensuring that no continuous perturbation (compression) can drive the system past a damaging one.

**Depth saturation under temporal integration is a design collision, not a bug.** The BG_DEPTH_WAIT condition was designed for a system where temporal_depth varies: low depth early in a waking period, high depth later. Under temporal integration (Day 33 packs active), depth fills to BUFFER_MAXLEN within 10 ticks and never recedes during a waking period. The depth axis was intended to distinguish early-waking from late-waking behavior; it instead becomes a permanent state. Pack 3 adapts the BG policy to this reality without modifying the depth gating (which is correct as a qualitative distinction: depth >= 5 = temporal structure available) by adding a sub-policy that uses state signals with real variance inside the gated regime.

**The system has now crossed the threshold from behavioral diversity to credit-stratified behavioral diversity.** Prior to Day 35, the organism produces multiple action types but credit is action-independent: the learning signal carries no information about which action was selected. After Day 35, credit is monotonically ordered by action type (46% spread, edit_code to explore). This is the precondition for behavioral adaptation: a policy that conditions on credit signal would receive meaningfully different gradient for each action type. The gradient exists; it is not yet consumed by any policy update mechanism.

## 9. Next Direction (NOT IMPLEMENTED)

The organism now has a non-flat, action-stratified credit signal. The ACS mean differs by action type by approximately 46% between the cheapest path (edit_code, via bound) and the most expensive (explore, via the depth-branch high-PE route). This gradient exists but is not consumed: no system reads the ACS trajectory and adjusts action selection probabilities, threshold parameters, or energy allocation accordingly. The next functional requirement is credit-to-decision coupling: a mechanism that allows the accumulated credit signal to influence the BG action selection policy in a controlled, bounded way. Specifically, a layer that modulates BG_TC_APPROACH or the PE threshold (currently 0.05) based on a short-horizon ACS moving average would allow the policy to shift toward explore during high-credit episodes and toward approach or idle_recover during low-credit episodes. The constraint is stability: coupling strength must be bounded (target influence <= 5-10% of threshold value) to prevent oscillation or collapse into a single-action attractor. This is the transition from a system that generates a learning signal to a system that acts on it.
