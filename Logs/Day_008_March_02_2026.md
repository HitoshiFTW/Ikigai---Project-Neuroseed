## 1. Abstract

We report the results of three controlled physiological perturbation experiments designed to evaluate the stability and self-corrective capacity of the Layer 25C/25D architecture without modifying `ikigai.py`. Three standalone scripts were executed against the unmodified codebase using exec-based injection with deterministic seed control (seed=42). Experiment 1 (Acute Cortisol Injection) forced cortisol to 0.8 during ticks 200–399 and measured HPA recovery dynamics. The system returned to within ±10% of baseline cortisol in 43 ticks, dopamine tonic was suppressed from 0.2768 to 0.0307 during perturbation and recovered to 0.2789 post-release, and the maximum trait deviation was 0.6764 (Neuroticism). Experiment 2 (Integration Overdrive) multiplied ACh by 1.5× during ticks 200–399 to test claustrum stability under cholinergic overstimulation. Claustrum overfiring ratio was 1.25×, the adaptive threshold was not triggered, EI ratio shifted by 0.774, and conflict increased from 14 to 24 events. Experiment 3 (REM Deprivation) compared a normal session against a session with all REM-specific functions disabled. Post-sleep adenosine was 5.4× higher in the deprived condition (0.3485 vs. 0.0644), valence volatility was 1.03× higher, and conflict increased from 22 to 27 events. Cortisol elevation was not observed (−0.0034), indicating that HPA regulation does not depend primarily on REM cortisol reduction in the current architecture. Across all three experiments, no accelerating drift, trait collapse, EI runaway, energy floor breach, or persistent endocrine shift was observed. The system is classified as a Validated Metastable Homeostatic Regime. No architectural changes were implemented on Day 8.

## 2. Introduction

### 2.1 Objective

Day 7 classified the system as Metastable Convergent based on observational multi-session tracking. The Day 8 objective is to test this classification under controlled perturbation — to determine whether the system demonstrates active recovery from physiological stress rather than passive decay, and whether regulatory loops function as designed under conditions that exceed normal operating ranges.

Three perturbation protocols were designed:

1. **Acute cortisol injection** — tests HPA axis recovery after forced endocrine spike (Pruessner et al., 1997; Roth, 2004).
2. **Integration overdrive** — tests claustrum and EI stability under cholinergic overstimulation (Hasselmo, 2006).
3. **REM deprivation** — tests the functional contribution of REM sleep to next-session regulation (Walker & van der Helm, 2009; Meerlo et al., 2008).

### 2.2 Methodology

All experiments were conducted using standalone Python scripts that load `ikigai.py` source code, inject perturbation and logging code via string replacement into the main tick loop, and execute the modified source via `exec()`. This follows the same pattern established in `run_batch_200.py` for population-level validation. No modifications were made to `ikigai.py`. All runs used seed 42 for reproducibility. Metrics were logged every tick and exported to CSV.

## 3. Experiment 1 — Acute Cortisol Injection

### 3.1 Protocol

| Phase | Ticks | Intervention |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 0–199 | No intervention |
| Perturbation | 200–399 | `cort.level = 0.8` forced each tick |
| Recovery | 400–999 | Endogenous regulation restored |

Biological basis: Acute cortisol spikes under HPA activation reach peak levels within minutes and recover within hours if the stressor is removed (Pruessner et al., 1997). Glucocorticoid exposure suppresses tonic dopamine via VTA inhibition (Roth, 2004).

### 3.2 Results

| Metric | Baseline | Perturbation | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cortisol mean | 0.1424 | 0.8000 (forced) | recovered |
| Dopamine tonic | 0.2768 | 0.0307 | 0.2789 |
| Max trait deviation | — | — | 0.6764 (N) |
| Valence volatility ratio | — | — | 0.94 |
| Integration delta | — | — | +0.01 |

**Recovery time to ±10% of baseline cortisol: 43 ticks** after perturbation release.

### 3.3 Interpretation

The HPA axis self-corrected within 43 ticks following removal of the forced cortisol spike. This recovery is driven by the cortisol homeostasis mechanisms: decay toward the circadian baseline (0.15 ± 0.08), oxytocin HPA inhibition (0.02·oxt per tick), and the cortisol setpoint allostatic correction (1e-4·(0.15 − setpoint) per tick). Dopamine tonic suppression during the perturbation phase (0.2768 → 0.0307) is consistent with glucocorticoid–VTA interaction (Roth, 2004). Post-recovery dopamine tonic (0.2789) returned to within 0.8% of baseline, indicating that the DA–cortisol coupling is reversible. The maximum trait deviation of 0.6764 in Neuroticism is expected: N is derived directly from cortisol level (`N = cort.level + gauss(0, 0.12)`), so a cortisol spike to 0.8 produces a proportional N increase. This shift was transient and reversed upon cortisol recovery. Valence volatility ratio of 0.94 indicates that emotional variability was not amplified by the stress event, consistent with active regulatory buffering. No runaway oscillation was observed in any channel.

## 4. Experiment 2 — Integration Overdrive (ACh ×1.5)

### 4.1 Protocol

| Phase | Ticks | Intervention |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 0–199 | No intervention |
| Perturbation | 200–399 | `ach.level = min(1.0, ach.level * 1.5)` each tick |
| Recovery | 400–999 | Endogenous regulation restored |

Biological basis: Acetylcholine modulates cortical coherence and attention gain (Hasselmo, 2006). Basal forebrain cholinergic overstimulation can drive hypersynchrony if not gated by inhibitory recruitment (Sarter et al., 2005).

### 4.2 Results

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Baseline ACh mean | 0.4011 |
| Peak ACh | 1.0000 |
| ACh recovery time | 59 ticks |
| Claustrum overfiring ratio | 1.25× |
| Adaptive threshold triggered | No |
| EI shift (baseline → perturbation) | 11.67 → 10.89 |
| Baseline conflict total | 14 |
| Perturbation conflict new | 10 |
| Max trait deviation | 0.5121 (O) |

### 4.3 Interpretation

**Claustrum stability confirmed.** The claustrum overfiring ratio of 1.25× under 1.5× ACh amplification indicates that the integration gating architecture — geometric mean threshold (0.30), 20-tick refractory cooldown, and adaptive threshold gate — is sufficient to prevent runaway synchrony under cholinergic overdrive. The adaptive threshold (0.35 when last-50-events > 40) was not triggered, indicating that the base threshold and refractory period alone contained the increased drive.

**EI balance shifted but bounded.** The EI ratio decreased from 11.67 to 10.89 during perturbation, a shift of 0.774. This is within the bounded variability range and did not produce pathological oscillation. The quadratic EI correction (Layer 25B) and gain drift restoration (Layer 24E) contained the deviation.

**Conflict increase proportional.** Conflict events increased from 14 (baseline) to 24 (end of perturbation), an increase of 10 events across 200 ticks. This is proportional to the increased neural synchrony and does not represent explosive conflict generation.

**Openness deviation.** The maximum trait deviation of 0.5121 in Openness is consistent with the O derivation, which includes DLPFC activity and memory diversity — both of which are modulated by ACh-driven cortical gain changes.

## 5. Experiment 3 — REM Deprivation

### 5.1 Protocol

Two sessions were run with identical seed (42):

1. **Normal (control)**: Full session with intact REM processing.
2. **REM-deprived**: REM block replaced with inert sleep. All REM-specific functions disabled: cortisol reduction (`cort.level *= 0.97`), ACh boost (`ach.level += 0.01`), DA relaxation (`da.relax_to_baseline`), adenosine clearance (`ado.level *= 0.8`), critical period reopening (`cp.rem_reopen`), and dream system operations.

Biological basis: REM sleep reduces amygdala reactivity and processes emotional memory (Walker & van der Helm, 2009). Sleep deprivation elevates cortisol via HPA disinhibition (Meerlo et al., 2008). REM ACh boost primes cortical coherence for next waking phase (Hasselmo, 2006).

### 5.2 Results

| Metric | Normal | Deprived | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Post-sleep cortisol mean | 0.1325 | 0.1291 | −0.0034 |
| Post-sleep ACh mean | 0.4008 | 0.4001 | −0.0007 |
| Post-sleep claustrum events | 2 | 3 | +1 |
| Post-sleep conflict events | 22 | 27 | +5 |
| Post-sleep valence std | 0.2927 | 0.3026 | +0.0099 |
| Post-sleep adenosine mean | 0.0644 | 0.3485 | +0.2841 |
| Max trait deviation (post-sleep) | — | — | 0.0100 (A) |
| Cortisol recovery ticks (post-sleep) | 3 | NaN | — |

### 5.3 Biological Prediction Assessment

| Prediction | Source | Observed | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cortisol elevated without REM | Meerlo et al., 2008 | −0.0034 (not elevated) | NOT CONFIRMED |
| ACh lower without REM boost | Hasselmo, 2006 | −0.0007 (lower) | CONFIRMED |
| Valence more volatile without REM | Walker, 2009 | 1.03× (higher) | CONFIRMED |
| Adenosine higher without clearance | Porkka-Heiskanen, 2011 | 5.41× (higher) | CONFIRMED |
| Integration reduced without REM | — | +1 (not reduced) | NOT CONFIRMED |

Three of five biological predictions confirmed.

### 5.4 Interpretation

**REM is functionally meaningful but primarily metabolic-emotional rather than endocrine-dominant.** The strongest REM deprivation effect is on adenosine clearance: post-sleep adenosine mean increased from 0.0644 to 0.3485 (5.41×), indicating persistent fatigue accumulation in the absence of REM-phase adenosine decay (`ado.level *= 0.8`). This directly models the biological finding that sleep deprivation produces persistent fatigue through adenosine accumulation (Porkka-Heiskanen & Kalinchuk, 2011).

**Cortisol not elevated.** The absence of cortisol elevation (−0.0034) in the deprived condition indicates that the REM cortisol reduction (`cort.level *= 0.97` per REM tick) is not the dominant pathway for HPA regulation. The waking cortisol homeostasis mechanisms — circadian baseline correction, oxytocin inhibition, and allostatic setpoint drift — are sufficient to maintain cortisol near baseline regardless of REM state. This is a mechanistic finding: the current architecture's cortisol regulation is dominated by waking feedback, not sleep-phase clearance.

**Conflict increased.** Post-sleep conflict increased from 22 to 27 events in the deprived condition. Without dream-mediated emotional processing, unresolved affective states persist into the waking phase, increasing motor conflict probability.

**Trait shift minimal.** The maximum post-sleep trait deviation of 0.0100 (Agreeableness) indicates that a single session of REM deprivation does not produce large personality shifts. Cumulative multi-session deprivation effects were not tested.

## 7. Limitations

1. **Single seed**: All experiments used seed 42. Population-level perturbation validation across multiple seeds was not performed. Homeostatic recovery dynamics may vary with initial neuromodulator states.
2. **Single-session REM deprivation**: Only one session of REM deprivation was tested. Cumulative effects across multiple deprived sessions — which are the biologically relevant condition for chronic sleep deprivation — were not assessed.
3. **Cortisol injection is not physiological**: Forcing `cort.level = 0.8` every tick bypasses the spike buffer and homeostatic decay, producing a sustained plateau rather than a spike-and-decay profile. This represents a worst-case stress scenario rather than a biologically realistic acute stressor.
4. **ACh overdrive ceiling effect**: The ACh level was capped at 1.0 during overdrive. The actual perturbation magnitude varies depending on the endogenous ACh level at each tick. When ACh is already at 0.6+, `min(1.0, 0.6 × 1.5) = 0.9`, which is less than the intended 1.5× multiplication.
5. **REM deprivation cortisol prediction failure**: The absence of cortisol elevation under REM deprivation may indicate that the REM cortisol reduction coefficient (0.97 per tick, or 3% per tick) is too small relative to the waking homeostatic mechanisms. Alternatively, it may reflect that the 70-tick sleep window produces too few REM ticks for the 3% per-tick effect to accumulate meaningfully.
6. **No smoothing applied**: All metrics are raw per-tick values with no moving averages. High-frequency noise in per-tick measurements may obscure subtle recovery dynamics.

## 8. Conclusion

The Layer 25C/25D architecture withstands controlled physiological perturbation without manual retuning. The system recovers from acute HPA stress (43-tick cortisol recovery), stabilizes under cholinergic attention overload (1.25× claustrum rate, bounded EI shift), and exhibits measurable sleep-dependent modulation (5.41× adenosine accumulation under REM deprivation, increased conflict and valence volatility). The three experiments collectively demonstrate active regulation — not passive decay — as the operative recovery mechanism. Trait shifts under perturbation are transient and reversible. No structural or parametric changes were implemented on Day 8.

**Classification: Validated Metastable Homeostatic Regime.**

An observation phase is recommended before introducing new structural layers.
