## 1. Objective

Day 33 introduces the temporal cognition layer to Ikigai: a four-pack cascade (TemporalCreditBridge, TemporalIntegrationLayer, SelectiveIgnitionLayer, DecisionBridgeLayer) that propagates multi-step outcome credit backward through time and selectively uses it to influence action binding decisions. A fifth corrective pack (ignition reparameterization) is introduced after behavioral analysis reveals that the initial ignition formula fires on continuous signal magnitude rather than discrete temporal events, collapsing the gate semantics. The overall objective is to give the organism a mechanism for distinguishing decisions that occur in the context of a recently-established reward sequence from decisions that occur in the absence of one -- and to allow that distinction to shift near-threshold action binding, within strictly bounded conditions, without replacing or overriding any prior regulatory architecture. All prior validation criteria (289 assertions across six experiment scripts) must be preserved across all packs.

## 2. Implemented Changes

**Pack 1 -- TemporalCreditBridge**

A bounded decay buffer accumulates outcome credit over successive reward events. On each waking tick, if the OutcomeEvaluator returns a nonzero actual reward, the buffer records the credit value. Buffer capacity is fixed at 10 positions. Each stored value is weighted by an exponential decay factor indexed to its position in the buffer. The sum of decay-weighted values is published as `temporal_credit_signal`; the current buffer length is published as `temporal_depth`. Both are stored in IkigaiContext. During sleep, the buffer is not cleared but no new entries are added; `temporal_depth` and `temporal_credit_signal` persist between ticks. The bridge is guarded by `enable_temporal_bridge` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The necessity: prior credit assignment (OCA) operates at single-tick resolution. No structure in the organism prior to Day 33 accumulates evidence that a reward sequence has been sustained over multiple steps, nor makes that evidence available to downstream systems.

**Pack 2 -- TemporalIntegrationLayer**

Combines the OCA single-tick credit with a small additive correction derived from the temporal buffer. The correction is computed as `clamp(temporal_credit_signal / BUFFER_MAXLEN, -1, 1) * INTEGRATION_ALPHA`, where `INTEGRATION_ALPHA = 0.12` (frozen). The sum `OCA.credit + temporal_correction` is published as `adjusted_credit_signal` in IkigaiContext. A sleeping guard zeros the output during sleep cycles. Guarded by `enable_temporal_integration`, defaulting to False.

The necessity: downstream layers (SIL, DBL) require a single credit signal that reflects both single-step outcome quality and multi-step temporal depth. TIL provides this combined signal without modifying OCA's internal formula.

**Pack 3 -- SelectiveIgnitionLayer**

Applies a gated additive bias to calibrated confidence. Bias formula: `signal = clamp(adjusted_credit_signal, -1, 1)`, `depth_factor = min(temporal_depth / 10.0, 1.0)`, `bias = signal * IGNITION_MAX_BIAS * depth_factor`. IGNITION_MAX_BIAS = 0.10 (frozen). Bias is added to calibrated confidence from ConfidenceCalibrator, not to raw confidence. The gate requires all of: `temporal_depth >= 3`, `0.3 <= base_confidence <= 0.8`, `strain_score < 0.75`, `mean_energy > 0.3`. The ignited result is clamped to [0, 1] and published as `ignited_confidence_signal`. During sleep or when disabled, the layer passes calibrated confidence through unchanged. Guarded by `enable_selective_ignition`, defaulting to False.

The necessity: a temporal context signal that never modulates downstream processing has no functional significance. SIL is the first transduction point where temporal credit affects the confidence computation. The additive, gated, bounded design prevents SIL from replacing calibration output with temporal output.

**Pack 4 -- DecisionBridgeLayer**

The first pack in which temporal structure can affect actual action selection. When all conditions are met, sets `override_bind_signal = True` in IkigaiContext. The call site in the main loop reads this flag before calling ActionBindingGate and temporarily lifts `action_confidence_evaluator.top_confidence` to at least `ActionBindingGate.BINDING_THRESHOLD` (0.60). ActionBindingGate itself is not modified. The gate requires all of: `top_confidence` in [DECISION_WINDOW_LOW=0.55, DECISION_WINDOW_HIGH=0.65] (frozen), `ignition_strong` (initial formulation: delta between ignited and top confidence exceeding IGNITION_THRESHOLD=0.05, frozen), `temporal_depth >= 3`, `strain_score < 0.75`, `mean_energy > 0.3`. Override resets to False at the top of every tick before re-evaluation.

Initial ignition condition: `ignition_delta = ignited_confidence_signal - top_confidence`. This formulation was found unreachable due to the calibration chain: the ConfidenceCalibrator applies `calibrated_conf = base_conf * (1 - uncertainty)`. The SIL adds a small positive bias. The net delta `ignited_conf - top_conf` is frequently negative because the uncertainty term suppresses the calibrated base more than the SIL bias recovers it. The gate therefore fired at ~0% of waking ticks.

**Pack 5 -- Ignition Reparameterization**

Replaces the delta-based ignition condition with a direct signal strength formulation: `signal_strength = abs(clamp(adjusted_credit_signal, -1, 1)) * depth_factor`. Gate condition: `signal_strength >= IGNITION_THRESHOLD = 0.05` (frozen, unchanged). This formulation was immediately superseded after behavioral analysis revealed 75-88% override frequency. Root cause: `adjusted_credit_signal` is dominated by OCA credit (~0.42) on every waking tick, and `temporal_depth` saturates at 10 on every waking tick due to OutcomeEvaluator returning nonzero actual reward continuously. `signal_strength` = 0.42 always, exceeding the threshold always.

**Pack 5 Fix -- Depth-Change Event Detection**

The signal_strength formula is replaced by a depth-change event detector. DecisionBridgeLayer gains `self._prev_depth = 0`. At each update: `depth_change = temporal_depth - self._prev_depth`, `event_strength = max(depth_change, 0)`, `ignition_strong = (event_strength >= 1)`. `self._prev_depth` is updated unconditionally at the end of each call. This gate fires only when `temporal_depth` increases relative to the prior tick -- that is, only when a new step has been added to the temporal buffer, indicating that a meaningful temporal event just occurred. When depth is stable (buffer saturated), the gate produces zero event strength and does not fire.

The necessity for this redesign: the organism operates in a continuous reward field. OutcomeEvaluator produces positive reward on every waking tick (empirically: 0.668-0.694 across all 500 measured waking ticks). As a result, the TCB buffer is always full, temporal_depth is always 10, and any magnitude-based gate using temporal_credit_signal or adjusted_credit_signal will fire continuously. The depth-change detector imposes temporal contrast that does not exist in the raw signals.

## 3. System Behavior (Post-Change)

With all four packs enabled and Pack 5 fix applied, the system exhibits sparse, event-correlated override activation. Override events occur in bursts at the start of each new reward sequence, as the TCB buffer ramps from its initial depth (at the first reward event after a period of silence) up to saturation. Each increment of temporal_depth by one unit triggers one override event. Once depth stabilizes at 10, no further depth-change events occur and the gate produces zero event strength. The system then remains silent until depth resets (which does not occur in the current configuration since actual_reward is nonzero every waking tick and depth remains at 10 persistently).

In the 2000-tick runs, 8 override events were recorded across the full run. In the 5000-tick runs, 8 override events were recorded. The burst occurs at run initialization as the TCB buffer fills for the first time; subsequent depth changes are zero because the buffer remains full. Depth distribution at override events: predominantly d=4 through d=10, each observed approximately once per run, consistent with a single ramp from depth 3 to depth 10 across 8 consecutive ticks.

Selective ignition (SIL) fires throughout waking periods when gates permit. Because `adjusted_credit_signal` is always approximately 0.42 and `temporal_depth` is always 10, the bias applied is consistently near `0.42 * 0.10 * 1.0 = 0.042` additive to calibrated confidence. This is a stable, near-constant upward adjustment to confidence during normal operation.

All systems remain dormant during sleep periods. IkigaiContext fields persist across sleep-wake transitions; the temporal buffer is not reset at sleep onset.

## 5. Key Findings

- **Continuous reward eliminates temporal contrast.** OutcomeEvaluator returns positive actual_reward (0.668-0.694) on every waking tick, causing the TCB buffer to remain full at all times after initialization. As a result, `temporal_depth` = 10 and `temporal_credit_signal` ≈ 3.55 are constant throughout all waking periods. No magnitude-based gate can distinguish temporal activity from temporal baseline under these conditions.

- **OCA credit dominates TIL output, masking temporal correction.** `adjusted_credit_signal` = OCA.credit + temporal_correction ≈ 0.28 + 0.14 = 0.42 uniformly. The temporal correction constitutes approximately 33% of the combined signal, but because both components are stable and nonzero throughout all waking ticks, the combined signal carries no event-boundary information.

- **Delta-based ignition (Pack 4 original) is structurally unreachable.** The ConfidenceCalibrator applies uncertainty suppression to calibrated confidence (`calibrated = base * (1 - uncertainty)`). SIL adds a positive bias to this suppressed value. The net difference `ignited_confidence - top_confidence` is negative or near-zero in practice because the uncertainty subtraction exceeds the SIL addition. The gate designed around this delta fires at approximately 0% of waking ticks, not because the temporal layer is inactive, but because the calibration chain algebraically prevents the delta from becoming positive.

- **Depth-change event detection produces sparse, burst-structured activation.** Replacing magnitude with `depth_change = temporal_depth - self._prev_depth` as the ignition signal yields events that are structurally tied to TCB buffer growth. The 8-event burst pattern observed (one event per depth increment from 3 to 10 at run initialization) reflects the single period of buffer ramp-up. After saturation, the gate produces silence for the remainder of the run. This is the correct behavioral property: events occur when temporal context changes, not when it persists.

- **Override does not alter binding frequency at the current operating point.** `top_confidence` is at or above 0.60 (BINDING_THRESHOLD) on 100% of waking ticks in both baseline and full modes. The override instruction, which lifts `top_confidence` to at least BINDING_THRESHOLD, has no marginal effect when the signal is already above that threshold. The temporal layer's influence on action selection is functionally zero in the current operating regime, not because the gate fails, but because the organism's confidence signal never falls into the sub-threshold range where the override would be decisive.

- **The organism lacks event-based temporal structure at the cognitive level.** A continuous reward field produces continuous depth, continuous signal, and continuous confidence -- all hovering near their equilibrium values. The temporal layer is architecturally present and correctly gated, but the organism does not produce the sparse reward events that would give temporal structure semantic content. This is not a failure of the temporal layer design; it is a characterization of the organism's current reward regime.

## 6. Failure Modes / Constraints

**Pack 4 ignition delta was algebraically unreachable.** The uncertainty correction in ConfidenceCalibrator suppresses calibrated confidence below the base confidence level. SIL's additive bias partially recovers this suppression but does not exceed it. The difference `ignited_confidence - top_confidence` is consistently negative or near-zero, making the `>= IGNITION_THRESHOLD` condition unsatisfiable. This was not detectable from the unit tests for Pack 4, which used stub objects with fixed confidence values and did not simulate the full calibration chain.

**Pack 5 magnitude formulation over-fired at 75-88%.** `signal_strength = abs(adjusted_credit_signal) * depth_factor = abs(0.42) * 1.0 = 0.42`, which exceeds `IGNITION_THRESHOLD = 0.05` on every waking tick. The formulation was correct in intent (use signal strength as ignition proxy) but incorrect in application to an organism where neither signal varies meaningfully between ticks. The diagnostic required probe-level measurement of actual_reward, OCA credit, and temporal_depth across 500 waking ticks to identify the root cause.

**l23.energy type mismatch in SIL and DBL.** The `_mean_energy()` helper was missing from the initial implementation of both layers. In real ikigai.py execution, `l23.energy` is a dict (`{cortex, limbic, motor}`). Direct float comparison of a dict raises TypeError. Unit tests used stub objects with float energy values and did not expose this. The bug was detected on first full exec() run. Three sites required correction: SelectiveIgnitionLayer, DecisionBridgeLayer, and TemporalCreditBridge (which had the isinstance check after the float() call, making the isinstance branch unreachable).

**Override provides no behavioral effect at the current operating point.** The temporal layer's decision-influencing capability is structurally present but operationally inactive because the organism's confidence never falls below BINDING_THRESHOLD without the override. This is a constraint imposed by the organism's current equilibrium, not by the temporal layer design. The layer is a conditional mechanism waiting for the operating regime to produce near-threshold confidence values.

**Entropy collapse pre-exists temporal layer activation.** Action entropy = 0.0000 in both baseline and full modes. This indicates that the waking action selection produces a single action type on every tick, a condition present before Day 33 and not caused by it. The entropy check in the health monitor flags this as a stabilization requirement, but the source is upstream of the temporal cognition layer.

## 7. Architectural Insights

**EEIL is a necessary but not sufficient condition for temporal cognition.** The EEIL architecture (alignment + regulation) prevents action degeneracy under continuous reward. It does not create temporal contrast. An organism in a high-density reward field with a well-tuned EEIL system will produce stable, non-degenerate behavior -- but all ticks will be structurally equivalent from a reward-signal perspective. Temporal cognition requires that some ticks carry more temporal information than others, which requires that reward or depth be episodic rather than continuous.

**The depth-change gate implements temporal contrast at the architectural level.** By gating on `temporal_depth - prev_depth >= 1` rather than on signal magnitude, DecisionBridgeLayer imposes an event-boundary structure that the raw signals do not provide. This is a design decision, not a measurement: the system defines "something meaningful just happened" as "the temporal context just deepened," regardless of whether the underlying reward signal was larger or smaller than usual. The gate fires on the derivative of depth, not on depth itself.

**Burst-structured ignition reflects a single TCB initialization event.** In the current organism, the buffer fills exactly once (at the start of each run) and never empties. The 8-event burst at depth 3-10 is therefore not a recurring pattern -- it is a one-time initialization artifact. A recurring burst pattern would require periodic TCB buffer drain, which in turn requires periods of zero actual_reward. The current organism does not produce such periods.

**The call-site override pattern correctly isolates ActionBindingGate from temporal structure.** The temporary lift of `action_confidence_evaluator.top_confidence` occurs between DecisionBridgeLayer.update() and ActionBindingGate.update(), within the same tick. ActionBindingGate reads the lifted value and makes its decision; on the next tick, `top_confidence` is recomputed from scratch by ActionConfidenceEvaluator before any override is applied. The temporal influence is strictly local to the tick in which the override fires, with no carry-forward. This design prevents the temporal layer from accumulating persistent influence on the confidence estimator's internal state.

**The temporal calibration chain has three suppressive steps.** (1) ConfidenceCalibrator applies uncertainty suppression: `calibrated = base * (1 - uncertainty)`. (2) SIL adds a bias that partially recovers suppression. (3) The net ignited confidence is still lower than or equal to the original `top_confidence` from ActionConfidenceEvaluator because ACE uses raw confidence, not calibrated confidence, for its top_confidence output. Any ignition gate that measures the delta between ignited confidence and top_confidence is comparing two quantities computed from different bases (calibrated vs. raw), making the delta dependent on calibration accuracy rather than on temporal signal strength.

**STRUCTURAL:** Four temporal systems added to the waking chain (TCB, TIL, SIL, DBL); IkigaiContext extended with 8 temporal fields (enable/signal pairs for all four packs); DecisionBridgeLayer holds one tick of persistent state (prev_depth) for event detection; all systems default to disabled (zero-drift when flags are off).

**TEMPORAL:** TCB buffer saturates at depth 10 within the first 10 reward events and remains saturated for the entire run; temporal_credit_signal stable at ~3.55 after initialization; adjusted_credit_signal stable at ~0.42; no episodic reward structure; burst-only ignition at run initialization.

**DECISION:** Override does not alter binding frequency at current operating point; top_confidence exceeds BINDING_THRESHOLD on 100% of waking ticks with or without the override; temporal layer has zero marginal effect on observed action selection in the current reward regime.

## 9. Next Direction (NOT IMPLEMENTED)

The organism requires episodic reward structure before the temporal cognition layer can produce semantically meaningful overrides. Currently, all waking ticks are reward-positive, which prevents temporal contrast from emerging at the signal level. Two directions address this:

The first direction is reward sparsification: introducing conditions under which the OutcomeEvaluator returns zero or near-zero actual reward. This would cause the TCB buffer to partially drain between reward sequences, producing temporal_depth values below 10 during quiescent periods and creating the contrast between sequence-onset ticks (rising depth) and inter-sequence ticks (stable or declining depth) that the depth-change gate requires to activate meaningfully.

The second direction is operating-point shift: bringing `top_confidence` into the [0.55, 0.65] window more frequently. The temporal layer's decision influence requires that the confidence signal be near-threshold. If the organism's equilibrium confidence is consistently above 0.65, the DECISION_WINDOW gate never opens regardless of ignition strength. This is distinct from the entropy collapse: bound fraction = 1.000 is consistent with top_confidence permanently above the window. Shifting the operating point requires changes to the action confidence estimation or the binding threshold -- neither of which should be made without understanding their consequences for the 289-assertion validation suite.

## 10. Verdict

**NEED STABILIZATION**

The temporal cognition layer is structurally complete and the Pack 5 depth-change fix resolves the critical over-firing condition (75-88% → 0.2-0.4%). However, the organism operates in a continuous reward field that prevents temporal contrast from emerging, making the temporal layer functionally inert at the current operating point. Additionally, action entropy = 0.0000 in both modes is a pre-existing instability that must be addressed before temporal cognition effects can be cleanly measured. The layer cannot be validated as functionally effective until the operating conditions produce episodic reward structure and near-threshold confidence values.
