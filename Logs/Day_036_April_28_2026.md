## 1. Objective

Day 36 addresses behavioral diversity in the context of a functioning learning signal. At the close of Day 35, the organism has a credit-stratified action distribution (ACS spread 46% across action types, std = 0.01637) but the BG system still selects from a narrow repertoire: approach dominates within the depth-branch sub-policy (88.6% of waking ticks), and exec_diversity -- the mapping from BG primitive names to execution-layer action types -- does not yet exist. The exec pipeline treats `approach` and `explore` as opaque terminal labels rather than dispatch indices into distinct execution behaviors. Additionally, the ACS signal is informative but not consumed: no component of the BG scoring path reads it to modulate action selection. The six-pack program for Day 36 is: (1) BG credit modulation -- wire ACS into the BG policy as a continuous score input; (2) execution diversity -- map BG primitive labels to execution-layer types (approach -> edit_code, explore -> run_experiment, wait/idle_recover -> idle_recover); (3) score-based BG selection -- replace the threshold if/elif chain with a continuous argmax over scored candidates; (4) BG score rebalancing -- equalize the base scale of the three scores so credit pressure is the differentiating signal; (5) credit-to-candidate coupling -- shift credit's influence from top_confidence (routing control) to candidate ranking within ACE; (6) PE exploration pressure -- apply LC-NE-inspired uncertainty pressure to the BG scoring block, boosting explore and suppressing approach when prediction error is high. All six packs must default to disabled with zero behavioral drift.

The central constraint: any credit mechanism that modifies top_confidence directly will raise TC above REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW (0.55) and render fallback_bg unreachable. This constraint is discovered empirically during Pack 1 development and shapes the architecture of every subsequent pack.

## 2. Implemented Changes

**Pack 1 -- Credit-to-Decision Coupling (DISABLED -- routing failure)**

The initial approach couples the ACS signal directly to ActionBindingGate's routing decision: high ACS -> multiplicative boost to top_confidence. The intent is to make successful behavioral sequences reinforce continued exploration by raising the confidence threshold closer to BINDING_THRESHOLD. The implementation raises top_confidence by `(1 + credit_effect)` where credit_effect is derived from ACS. Diagnostic run over 500 waking ticks with Pack 1 enabled shows TC mean rising from 0.5074 to 0.5623 -- a 0.055 increase that crosses REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW (0.55). With TC at 0.5623, ActionBindingGate routes all sub-threshold traffic to fallback_reasoning (which returns edit_code from ACE) rather than fallback_bg. The BG pathway receives 0% of waking ticks; fallback_bg is structurally unreachable. Pack 1 is disabled permanently and excluded from all subsequent pack configurations. `enable_credit_coupling` remains in IkigaiContext as a diagnostic flag defaulting to False; it is never set to True in any further experiment.

**Pack 2 -- BG Credit Modulation and Execution Diversity**

Wires the ACS signal into the BG decision path as a normalized scalar `_credit_n = (ACS + 1.0) / 2.0 ∈ [0, 1]` and introduces the exec_diversity mapping layer. The exec_diversity block runs in the waking-phase main loop after ActionBindingGate.update() and writes to `_IKG.exec_type` without modifying `bound_action` in place: approach -> edit_code, explore -> run_experiment, wait -> idle_recover, idle_recover -> idle_recover. The map preserves all downstream system reads of bound_action (which observe the BG primitive label) while providing exec_type for external metrics and reward attribution. Pack 2 is guarded by `enable_bg_credit` and `enable_exec_diversity` in IkigaiContext, both defaulting to False.

**Pack 3 -- Score-Based BG Selection**

Replaces the threshold if/elif chain inside the `depth >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT` branch of ActionBindingGate with a continuous score argmax over three candidates: explore, approach, wait. Base scores are derived from signal thresholds (PE signal for explore, top_conf for approach, depth for wait) plus a credit modulation term `_k * credit_modifier` per candidate. The winner is `bg_name = max(_bg_scores, key=_bg_scores.get)`. Pack 3 is guarded by `enable_bg_scoring`, defaulting to False.

**Pack 4 -- BG Score Rebalancing**

The Pack 3 base scores contain tautological conditions: `(0.6 if top_conf < BG_TC_APPROACH else 0.3)` for approach, `(0.6 if bg_pe > 0.05 else 0.3)` for explore. With TC compressed to ~0.504 by Day 35 Pack 2, approach always satisfies `top_conf < BG_TC_APPROACH` and receives base 0.6 unconditionally. Prediction error is near-zero (mean 0.009) on the vast majority of ticks, so explore almost always fails `bg_pe > 0.05` and receives base 0.3. The 0.3 gap between approach and explore base scores is larger than any credit modulation can overcome at _k = 0.3 and typical ACS values. Pack 4 equalizes all three base scores to 0.45, removing the conditional structure: `_sc_explore = 0.45 + _k * (1.0 - _credit_n)`, `_sc_approach = 0.45 + _k * _credit_n`, `_sc_wait = 0.45 + 0.05 * (0.5 - abs(_credit_n - 0.5))`. Credit pressure becomes the sole differentiating signal. Pack 4 is guarded by `enable_bg_rebalance`, defaulting to False.

**Pack 5 -- Credit-to-Candidate Coupling**

Moves credit's influence from top_confidence (routing control layer) to candidate ranking within ActionConfidenceEvaluator. The mechanism is mean-preserving additive perturbation: (1) capture the mean of `adjusted_confidence` across all candidates before modification; (2) add a per-candidate delta based on whether the candidate's action_type matches `_IKG.last_action_type` (the previous tick's selected action) -- the matching candidate receives `+MAX_DELTA * credit_n * depth_factor`, all others receive `-MAX_DELTA * 0.25 * depth_factor`; (3) re-center all candidates by subtracting the drift `(mean_after - mean_before)`; (4) clamp each adjusted_confidence to [0.0, 1.0]; (5) re-sort candidates. MAX_DELTA = 0.02, depth_factor = min(temporal_depth / 10.0, 1.0). top_confidence is set from `scored[0].get('adjusted_confidence')` at line ~22575, before any Pack 5 code runs; Pack 5 does not touch this assignment. top_confidence is intentionally not modified. Pack 5 is guarded by `enable_credit_selection`, defaulting to False.

The multiplicative approach (Pack 1's method) was rejected for a structural reason: `adjusted_confidence` of the top candidate feeds `estimated_impact` in subsequent ticks. `estimated_impact` enters the base confidence computation. Global scaling raises the feedback loop input, which raises top_confidence through base_conf, which crosses 0.55 regardless of Pack 1 being named as the cause. The mean-preserving additive approach changes rank without changing scale, leaving the mean of the confidence distribution unchanged and TC at its natural ~0.507.

**Pack 6 -- PE Exploration Pressure**

Applies PE-driven exploration pressure inside the BG scoring block, after base scores and credit modulation are set, before the argmax. The block activates when `enable_pe_exploration` is True: `_pe_bg = float(_IKG.bg_pe)` (prediction error injected per tick from `pp.error` after `pred_err = pp.update(signal)`); `_sc_explore += 0.18 * _pe_bg`; `_sc_approach -= 0.108 * _pe_bg`; `_sc_wait += 0.04 * (0.5 - abs(_pe_bg - 0.5))`; all scores clamped to >= 0. The biological motivation: LC-NE activity encodes environmental uncertainty and drives exploration over exploitation when prediction error is high. K_PE = 0.18, suppression coefficient = 0.6 * K_PE. Pack 6 is guarded by `enable_pe_exploration`, defaulting to False.

The initial Pack 6 implementation is applied on top of the Pack 4 base scores with their tautological conditional structure still in place. With the conditionals active, Pack 6 has no practical effect: approach base = 0.6 unconditionally, explore base = 0.3 unconditionally; K_PE = 0.18 on PE mean = 0.009 produces explore boost of 0.0016 against a 0.3 gap. K_PE required to bridge the gap at mean PE: 0.3 / 0.009 / 0.288 = 115.7 (infeasible). The fix is Pack 4 base equalization -- once all bases are 0.45, the gap shrinks to 0.098 (at ACS mean = 0.326, credit_n = 0.663) and PE pressure becomes a real, if not sufficient at mean, differentiating signal.

## 5. Key Findings

- **Credit-to-TC coupling is structurally incompatible with BG routing.** Any mechanism that raises top_confidence as a function of credit will eventually cross REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW (0.55) under sustained positive ACS. At ACS mean = 0.467 (Day 35 baseline), even a 0.05 multiplicative boost produces TC = 0.507 * 1.1 = 0.558, crossing the threshold. Once TC crosses 0.55, fallback_reasoning intercepts all sub-threshold traffic and returns edit_code from ACE. The BG pathway cannot activate. The constraint is not tunable by reducing the boost coefficient: credit is positive on average (positive ACS = rewarded actions), so any positive coupling will drift TC upward over time. The correct intervention point is below TC -- within the candidate ranking layer (ACE) where credit perturbs which candidate is selected without touching the value that routing reads.

- **Mean-preserving perturbation is the necessary form of credit influence on confidence.** Multiplicative scaling of adjusted_confidence propagates through the feedback loop: adjusted_confidence -> estimated_impact -> base_conf -> top_confidence. Global scale changes raise the mean of the confidence distribution; the mean propagates via feedback to TC. Additive perturbation that sums to zero across candidates changes rank without changing mean. The re-centering step (subtract `mean_after - mean_before` from all candidates) is essential: without it, a net-positive set of deltas raises the mean and replicates the feedback loop failure of Pack 1. The re-centering converts a perturbation from "scale change" to "rank change." This distinction is the architectural invariant that allows credit to influence behavior while preserving routing stability.

- **Base score asymmetry renders PE pressure inoperable.** With conditional base scores -- approach receiving 0.6 when TC < 0.53 (always true at TC = 0.504), explore receiving 0.3 when PE > 0.05 (almost never true at PE mean = 0.009) -- the base gap is 0.3. K_PE = 0.18 on PE mean = 0.009 produces a net score shift of 0.288 * 0.009 = 0.0026, three orders of magnitude below the gap. No continuous PE pressure mechanism can overcome a structural asymmetry in base allocation. The fix is base equalization: identical 0.45 base for all actions, credit as the sole static differentiator. The resulting gap at observed ACS is 0.098, which PE can partially bridge at high-PE ticks (max observed shift = 0.037). The behavioral change is real: edit_code drops from 97-100% to 63.8% after equalization. The source of that shift is not explore winning the scoring block -- it is the energy depletion gate (bg_energy < 0.30) routing 36% of BG ticks to idle_recover, which is driven by credit-unrelated energy dynamics. Explore does not yet win the scoring block under observed signal ranges.

- **The energy depletion gate creates emergent diversity without requiring scoring block competition.** Energy mean = 0.318 is close to BG_ENERGY_LOW = 0.30; energy variance (std not directly measured, but min = 0.000 observed) causes frequent crossings of the 0.30 threshold. Each crossing bypasses the scoring block entirely and routes to idle_recover. This produces the idle_recover surge (2.6% PE off -> 32.7% PE on in 2000t run). The PE-on condition does not directly change the energy depletion rate; rather, the PE-on configuration uses the equalized base (Pack 4 post-fix), which produces different energy consumption patterns than the pre-fix conditional base. The depletion-driven diversity is behaviorally real but architecturally fragile: it depends on the proximity of mean energy to BG_ENERGY_LOW rather than on signal-driven scoring competition.

- **Routing attribution requires the binding_path attribute, not action_id prefix inspection.** Both bound and fallback_reasoning paths produce action_ids from the ACE candidate pool; neither uses the `bg_` prefix that fallback_bg attaches. Initial METRICS_CODE using `if _aid.startswith('bg_')` classified all non-BG ticks as bound (100%), masking the distinction between fallback_reasoning (98% in Pack 1 config) and genuine bound (2%). The fix reads `str(getattr(action_binding_gate, 'binding_path', 'unknown'))` which explicitly records the routing decision string as set by ABG: 'bound', 'fallback_reasoning', or 'fallback_bg'. Pack 1's failure was initially misdiagnosed as bound=100% before this fix revealed the actual fallback_reasoning=98% routing.

- **ACS variance has increased monotonically through the Day 36 pack sequence.** Day 35 full: std = 0.01637. Pack 5 alone (5000t): std = 0.01723. Pack 6 full (5000t): std = 0.02282. The increase reflects a broadening of the action distribution: with two exec_types (edit_code, idle_recover) rather than effectively one, the OutcomeEvaluator receives more variable energy_delta inputs, producing more variable actual_reward, propagating to higher ACS variance. Credit is becoming increasingly expressive as diversity increases.

## 6. Failure Modes / Constraints

**Pack 1 TC drift.** Detailed above in Key Findings. The mechanism: multiplicative TC boost crosses REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW (0.55), ABG routes to fallback_reasoning instead of fallback_bg, BG receives zero traffic, all behavioral diversity collapses to edit_code. The misdiagnosis (bound=100%) delayed identification by one diagnostic cycle; the routing attribution fix revealed fallback_reasoning=98% as the actual collapse mechanism. Pack 1 is permanently disabled.

**Pack 6 initial implementation -- base asymmetry.** With Pack 4's conditional base scores active (before equalization), Pack 6 produces no measurable behavioral change. V5.3 (entropy > 0.3), V5.4 (run_experiment > 5%), and V5.5 (edit_code < 85%) all fail with entropy = 0.1744 and edit_code = 97-98%. The root cause is not K_PE being too small: the base gap of 0.3 requires K_PE > 115 at mean PE to bridge, and K_PE > 1.95 at maximum observed PE (0.126). Raising K_PE beyond 0.2 risks destabilizing the scoring block and violates the design constraint. The architectural fix (base equalization in Pack 4) reduces the gap to 0.098, enabling PE pressure to have a non-negligible effect. The Pack 6 implementation (K_PE = 0.18) is correct given the fixed base; the failure was a dependency ordering issue -- Pack 6 requires Pack 4 base equalization as a prerequisite.

**run_experiment residual failure.** After base equalization, explore does not win the scoring block at observed signal values. At ACS mean = 0.326 (credit_n = 0.663): approach score = 0.649, explore score = 0.551, gap = 0.098. Maximum PE-driven swing: 0.288 * 0.112 = 0.032 (less than the 0.098 gap). Explore would win when credit_n < (0.3 + 0.288 * pe) / 0.6, approximately < 0.524 at mean PE -- requiring ACS < 0.048. ACS mean = 0.326, std = 0.023; the required ACS value is 12 standard deviations below the mean. Explore cannot win the scoring block under observed conditions. The idle_recover diversity (36%) comes from the energy depletion gate, not from explore selection. V5.4 (run_experiment > 5%) fails in Pack 6.

Pack 5 V6.6 and V6.7 failures (run_experiment = 0.0%, edit_code = 88.3%) are correctly attributed: both are diversity targets that require Pack 6 PE pressure to be effective. Without Pack 6 or with Pack 6 on pre-equalization base, these targets are unachievable within the current scoring architecture.

## 7. Architectural Insights

**Credit must act on rank, not on scale.** The BG routing system separates into two subsystems: a routing gate (TC-based, threshold at 0.55/0.60) and a selection policy (score argmax among BG candidates). Credit influencing the routing gate raises TC, which blocks BG access -- exactly the opposite of the intended effect. Credit influencing candidate ranking within ACE changes which action the organism chooses without affecting which pathway the routing gate activates. The distinction is between a global scaling operation (which changes the absolute level of the confidence distribution) and a relative ranking operation (which permutes candidates without changing their mean). The mean-preserving constraint is the formal statement of this architectural requirement: rank changes are admissible; scale changes are not.

**Threshold-based multi-axis policies degenerate to single-axis policies when one axis saturates.** The BG scoring block has three axes: energy (gate), depth (gate), and then PE + TC (scoring). Day 33 temporal integration saturates depth to BUFFER_MAXLEN = 10 within 10 waking ticks; depth is always >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT thereafter. The energy gate fires on approximately 36% of ticks at current energy levels. The combined result is that approximately 64% of BG ticks reach the scoring block; within the scoring block, credit is the sole dynamic signal (PE mean = 0.009 is too small to reliably differentiate). A scoring architecture where one axis (credit) consistently points in one direction (positive ACS -> approach) will produce near-monoculture output from the scoring block. Breaking this requires either introducing signal variance (reducing credit's dominance) or making the gated-out paths (explore, run_experiment) accessible without requiring the scoring block to select them.

**Behavioral diversity can emerge from boundary conditions rather than from policy competition.** The observed idle_recover increase (2.6% to 36%) is driven by energy proximity to BG_ENERGY_LOW, not by explore winning the scoring block. The energy boundary is a hard structural gate that bypasses policy entirely. Diversity from boundary conditions is fragile (it depends on energy dynamics remaining near the threshold) and coincidental (idle_recover is the output, not the intended explore). Robust diversity requires the scoring block to actually select non-dominant alternatives, which requires either credit variance sufficient to bring credit_n below 0.5 on some ticks, or PE signal strong enough to overcome the credit gap on high-uncertainty ticks.

**The learning signal is now structurally capable of driving behavioral change.** ACS std has increased from 0.01637 (Day 35) to 0.02282 (Day 36, all packs), a 39% increase. Credit is action-type-stratified and routing-path-sensitive. The mean-preserving perturbation ensures credit modulates candidate selection within ACE without loop-back to TC. The exec_diversity layer gives BG primitives concrete semantic content (approach = code modification, explore = experiment execution). The system now has: a learning signal (ACS), a transmission mechanism (Pack 5 candidate perturbation), and an execution vocabulary (exec_diversity). What it lacks is sufficient signal strength at the scoring boundary to make explore competitive with approach under typical credit conditions.

## 9. Next Direction (NOT IMPLEMENTED)

The explore -> run_experiment path is structurally complete (exec_diversity mapping correct, scoring block position correct, PE signal correctly injected) but explore does not win the scoring block. The credit gap at ACS mean (credit_n = 0.663) is 0.098. Two mechanisms could bring explore into competition:

First, credit variance: if ACS were to vary enough that credit_n fell below 0.524 on some ticks, explore would win without any PE. ACS std = 0.023 gives credit_n std = 0.012; credit_n would need to reach 0.524, which is 11.5 standard deviations below mean. This requires either negative ACS episodes (actions producing negative actual_reward) or a fundamentally different action-outcome reward structure. The current reward model does not produce sustained negative ACS; approach consistently earns positive credit via its energy_delta bias.

Second, credit pressure reduction: reducing _k from 0.3 to a value where the credit gap at mean ACS is bridgeable by PE at the 90th percentile of observed PE values. At the 90th percentile PE (approximately 0.022), PE swing = 0.288 * 0.022 = 0.006. For K_PE to bridge a gap of 0.006, _k must produce a gap of 0.006 at credit_n = 0.663: `0.3 * (2*0.663 - 1) = 0.3 * 0.326 = 0.098`. Reduction of _k to 0.05 would give gap = 0.016, bridgeable by PE at the 95th percentile. The tradeoff is reduced credit influence on scoring, which may eliminate the credit-to-behavior causal chain that Pack 5 was designed to establish.

The deeper structural question is whether the scoring block is the correct location for behavioral diversification. The energy depletion gate already produces 36% diversity without any scoring competition. Extending the gate to include a probabilistic explore channel during high-PE episodes (PE-conditioned gate rather than energy-conditioned gate) would produce run_experiment without requiring explore to beat approach in continuous scoring. This approach decouples the diversity mechanism from the credit competition mechanism and allows each to be tuned independently.

The routing architecture is stable: TC = 0.5037, fallback_bg = 99.36% over 5000 ticks, zero TC drift from credit operations. The PE exploration pressure (Pack 6) is structurally correct and activates inside the BG scoring block as specified. Twenty of twenty-one Pack 6 assertions pass; twenty-five of twenty-seven Pack 5 assertions pass. The single remaining failure (run_experiment = 0.0%) reflects a credit-dominance problem in the scoring block: approach wins under all observed credit and PE signal combinations. The system is ready for a targeted intervention at the scoring boundary to make explore competitive.
