## 3. System 1 -- Hunger / Metabolic Drive

### 3.1 Biological Basis

Berridge (2009) dissociates incentive salience ("wanting") from hedonic impact ("liking").
Dopamine in the nucleus accumbens and VTA encodes wanting -- the motivational drive toward
reward -- not the pleasure of reward itself. Hunger is a metabolic signal that activates the
mesolimbic dopamine pathway, creating urgency and approach behavior independent of explicit
reward prediction. Animals deprived of food show elevated DA firing in the VTA before any
food is present. The metabolic signal is hypothalamic (arcuate nucleus ghrelin → lateral
hypothalamus → VTA → NAc).

## 4. System 2 -- Circadian Rhythm Regulation

### 4.1 Biological Basis

Borbely (1982) two-process model: sleep is governed by Process S (homeostatic pressure,
adenosine substrate) and Process C (circadian oscillator, suprachiasmatic nucleus). Process C
creates an approximately 24h alertness rhythm independent of prior sleep duration. The morning
cortisol awakening response is a well-documented Process C signature. At the circadian trough,
sleep drive is augmented even if homeostatic Process S pressure is low (Dijk & Czeisler 1995).

### 4.2 Implementation

**Constants** (before main loop):
```python
CIRCADIAN_PERIOD = 1000  # ticks per full circadian cycle
_CIRC_OFFSET = 250       # phase offset: tick 0 at peak wake alertness
```

**Per-tick signal** (at tick start, before sleep transition):
```python
_circadian_signal = math.sin((tick + _CIRC_OFFSET) * (2 * math.pi / CIRCADIAN_PERIOD))
```
Range: [-1, +1]. Positive = wake-promoting phase. Negative = sleep-promoting phase.

**Three downstream effects:**

**(a) Circadian cortisol rhythm** (after avg_energy computation):
```python
cort.level = min(1.0, max(0.0, cort.level + _circadian_signal * 0.02))
```

**(b) Sleep drive modulation** (after predictive sleep merge):
```python
homeostasis.drives['sleep'] = min(1.0, max(0.0,
    homeostasis.drives['sleep'] - _circadian_signal * 0.20))
```
During negative circadian phase (night): sleep drive boosted by up to +0.20.
During positive phase (day): sleep drive suppressed by up to -0.20.

**(c) Interaction with probabilistic sleep gate (Gap 3):** circadian cortisol rhythm
interacts with the probabilistic gate, making sleep harder during the circadian alerting window.

### 4.3 Phase Mapping

| Tick range (within period) | Circadian signal | Biological phase |
|---------------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| 0-249   | Rising, ~0 to +1 | Mid-morning to peak alertness |
| 250-499 | Falling, +1 to 0 | Afternoon |
| 500-749 | Negative         | Sleep-promoting trough |
| 750-999 | Rising, -1 to 0  | Early morning recovery |

## 6. System 4 -- Allostatic Survival Regulation

### 6.1 Biological Basis

McEwen & Stellar (1993) defined allostatic load as the cumulative physiological cost of
adaptation. Chronically elevated allostatic load produces: increased amygdala reactivity,
reduced hippocampal volume, elevated baseline HPA activity, and reduced prefrontal executive
function. Joels & Baram (2009) showed that chronic corticosterone reduces BDNF and Arc
expression, impairing synaptic plasticity. The existing `AllostasisSystem` (Day 14) tracked
cumulative load but did not feed back into energy-metabolism or plasticity equations.

### 6.2 Implementation

**(a) Allostatic alpha amplification** (after Law 5, waking branch):
```python
# Day 17 System 4: Allostatic load amplifies metabolic cost (McEwen & Stellar 1993)
alpha = alpha * (1.0 + allostasis.allostatic_load * 0.30)
```

Combined with Law 5: `alpha_effective = 0.025 * (1 + cort*0.5) * (1 + load*0.30)`.
At chronic stress (cort=0.40, load=0.50): alpha x1.20 x1.15 = x1.38.
At extreme state (cort=0.60, load=1.0): alpha x1.30 x1.30 = x1.69.

**(b) Allostatic pmod suppression** -- combined with System 3 (see section 5.3 above).

### 6.3 Allostatic Collapse Cascade

The allostatic cascade forms a true positive feedback loop under chronic stress:

```
Sustained cortisol > 0.6
    |-> allostasis.allostatic_load accumulates (Day 14 mechanism)
    |-> System 4: alpha amplified (+30% per unit load)
    |-> energy depletes faster
    |-> Law 2: cortisol rises further
    |-> Law 5: alpha amplified further
    |-> allostatic load accumulates faster
    |-> System 3 + System 4: pmod suppressed (learning degraded)
```

This is the first time NeuroSeed models allostatic collapse rather than just allostatic load.

## 9. System 6 -- LC-NE Threat / Arousal

### 9.1 Biological Basis

The locus coeruleus is the brain's primary norepinephrine source, projecting widely across
cortex, limbic system, and brainstem. It acts as a global arousal and attentional gain
modulator. When threat is detected via ascending PE signals and cortical alarm signals, LC
firing rate increases, causing: global cortical gain enhancement, sleep suppression through
inhibition of the ventrolateral preoptic area (VLPO), increased metabolic expenditure, and
suppression of exploratory behaviour in favour of threat-focused vigilance (Aston-Jones &
Cohen 2005). Noradrenergic arousal is triggered when a combined threat signal -- weighted
equally between prediction error (novelty) and cortisol (stress) -- exceeds 0.25. The arousal
signal decays exponentially with a ~14-tick half-life (factor 0.95/tick).

### 9.2 Implementation

**State variable initialisation** (after `_CIRC_OFFSET = 250`):
```python
_arousal_signal = 0.0   # LC-NE noradrenergic arousal level [0, 1]
_threat_level   = 0.0   # combined threat perception [0, 1]
```

**Per-tick decay** (after `_circadian_signal = math.sin(...)`):
```python
_arousal_signal = max(0.0, _arousal_signal * 0.95)   # ~14-tick half-life
homeostasis._arousal_override = _arousal_signal > 0.30
_threat_level = 0.0; _action_cost = 0.0; _policy_score = 0.0
```

**Threat detection and neural gain** (after `pred_err = pp.update(signal)`):
```python
_threat_level = min(1.0, 0.5 * pp.error + 0.5 * cort.level)
if _threat_level > 0.25:
    _arousal_signal = min(1.0, _arousal_signal + (_threat_level - 0.25) * 1.5)
pp.error = min(1.0, pp.error * (1.0 + _arousal_signal * 0.4))
```

The neural gain amplification models LC-NE signal-to-noise enhancement: under arousal, the
same sensory discrepancy is processed as more salient. Threshold requires both PE and cortisol
to be elevated -- pure metabolic burden without novelty does not activate LC-NE (biologically
correct: a person collapsing from exhaustion does not become more vigilant).

**Arousal firing cost** (after allostatic alpha line):
```python

```

**Arousal sleep override** in `should_sleep_onset()` (after probabilistic gate):

## 11. System 8 -- Action-Outcome World Model and Agency

### 11.1 Biological Basis

In mammalian neuroscience, the distinction between habitual and goal-directed action is
well-established (Rangel et al. 2008; Balleine & O'Doherty 2010):

| System | Neural substrate | Computation | Ikigai equivalent |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Habitual | Dorsal striatum / BG | Stimulus-response | `bg_sys.select_action()` |
| Goal-directed | Prefrontal cortex | Action-outcome model | Day 19 world model |
| Motivation | VTA/dopamine | Reward weighting | `da.level` modulation |
| Interoception | Hypothalamus | Energy/need state | Homeostasis drives |

Friston (2010) active inference: actions are selected to minimise expected free energy --
a quantity proportional to prediction error (surprise) plus metabolic cost.

### 11.2 Survival Value Function

```
SV = w_e * predicted_energy
   - w_pe * log(1 + predicted_PE)
   - w_cort * predicted_cortisol
   - w_cost * action_cost
```

The logarithmic PE term models diminishing information utility: large PE reductions are more
valuable than small ones, preventing tiny PE fluctuations from dominating action decisions.

### 11.3 Architecture

```
Interoception -> Drives -> Basal Ganglia (habitual policy)
                                  |
                Action-Outcome World Model (deliberative evaluation)
                                  |
                if DeltaSV > 0.02: override BG selection
                                  |
                Final action selection -> executed
```

The BG remains the primary system (wins unless world model finds ΔSV > 0.02). This mirrors
cortical suppression of BG in mammalian goal-directed behaviour (Valentin et al. 2007).

## 12. System 9 -- Development Metrics: Learning, Maturity, Wisdom

### 12.1 Biological Basis

Friston (2010) predictive learning: learning is best measured as cumulative reduction in
prediction error over the organism's active history. Maturity can be operationalised as
behavioral policy stability -- a mature organism shows lower action entropy, reflecting a
stable and contextually appropriate behavioral repertoire. Wisdom is a concept grounded in
Sterling & Eyer (1988) allostasis and Damasio (1999) somatic regulation: the wisest organisms
maintain physiological stability across diverse conditions, reflected in low variance of energy
and cortisol over time.

### 12.2 DevelopmentMetrics Class

Implemented as a permanent class in `ikigai.py` before the PredictiveSleepSystem block:

```python
class DevelopmentMetrics:
    """
    Tracks three developmental dimensions of the organism:

    Learning  -- cumulative reduction in prediction error (Friston 2010)
    Maturity  -- stability of behavioral policy, inverse of action entropy
    Wisdom    -- homeostatic stability: inverse of energy+cortisol variance
    """

    def __init__(self):
        self.learning_progress = 0.0
        self.maturity = 0.0
        self.wisdom = 0.0
        self.prev_pe = None
        self.prev_actions = []
        self.pe_history = []
        self.energy_history = []
        self.cort_history = []

    def update_learning(self, pe):
        if self.prev_pe is not None:
            delta = self.prev_pe - pe
            self.learning_progress += max(0.0, delta)
        self.prev_pe = pe
        self.pe_history.append(pe)

    def update_maturity(self, action):
        if action is None:
            return
        self.prev_actions.append(action)
        if len(self.prev_actions) > 200:
            self.prev_actions.pop(0)
        counts = {}
        for a in self.prev_actions:
            counts[a] = counts.get(a, 0) + 1
        probs = [v / len(self.prev_actions) for v in counts.values()]
        entropy = -sum(p * math.log(p + 1e-9) for p in probs)
        self.maturity = 1.0 / (1.0 + entropy)

    def update_wisdom(self, energy, cortisol):
        self.energy_history.append(energy)
        self.cort_history.append(cortisol)
        if len(self.energy_history) > 300:
            self.energy_history.pop(0)
            self.cort_history.pop(0)
        if len(self.energy_history) < 10:
            return
        energy_var = statistics.pvariance(self.energy_history)
        cort_var   = statistics.pvariance(self.cort_history)
        self.wisdom = 1.0 / (1.0 + energy_var + cort_var)
```

## 13. Sleep Architecture Diagnosis and Repair

### 13.1 Context

Prior to the sleep architecture audit, the organism exhibited 94-97% sleep rate under natural
homeostasis -- biologically implausible for any mammal. Systematic diagnosis identified three
distinct and cascading bugs.

### 13.2 Bug 1 -- Permanent Sleep Attractor (Adenosine Never Clears During Sleep)

**Root cause:** `ado.update()` and `homeostasis.update()` were called exclusively inside the
`if not sleeping:` waking branch. During sleep, adenosine accumulated without any clearance
mechanism. The `should_sleep_end()` method checks `drives["sleep"] < SLEEP_OFFSET_THRESHOLD`,
which was never True because the drive was frozen at the onset value (>0.80). The organism
could never wake up.

**Diagnosis:** Diagnostic injection confirmed that PSP (predictive sleep pressure) and
`drives["sleep"]` both remained frozen at onset values throughout the sleeping branch,
with ado.level capped at 1.0. Zero ticks of `should_sleep_end() == True` were recorded.

**Fix:** Added sleep-branch adenosine clearance and direct drive management:
```python
    if sleeping:
        for k in l23.energy: l23.energy[k] = min(1.0, l23.energy[k] + 0.01)
        # Day 19.5/19.6 -- Sleep-branch adenosine clearance (Borbely 1982 Process S)
        # Without this, drives["sleep"] is frozen at onset value -> organism never wakes.
        # Rate 0.98 per tick -> ~45 ticks from 0.5 to below 0.20
        ado.level = max(0.0, ado.level * 0.98)
        Synapse.ado_level = ado.level
        if ado.level >= 0.20:
            homeostasis.drives['sleep'] = 0.50   # maintain sleep
        else:
            homeostasis.drives['sleep'] = 0.0    # adenosine cleared -> wake next tick
```

**Why not call homeostasis.update() during sleep:** Calling the full `homeostasis.update()`
during sleep set `drives["sleep"]` to the raw adenosine pressure (~0.10), immediately below
`SLEEP_OFFSET_THRESHOLD` (0.30), ending sleep after only 1 tick. Directly managing
`drives["sleep"]` in the sleeping branch provides the correct biological behaviour: sleep
is maintained while adenosine remains elevated, and terminates when it clears.

### 13.3 Bug 2 -- Multiplicative Wake Drive Prevents Sleep Onset

**Root cause:** The initial wake drive implementation multiplied sleep pressure by a
suppression factor: `drives['sleep'] *= (1 - wake_factor)`. With `wake_factor = 0.275`
at baseline energy (common during normal operation), the formula yielded
`1.0 * (1 - 0.275) = 0.725` -- below the 0.80 onset threshold. Onset was mathematically
impossible during normal physiology.

**Biological correction (user feedback):** Real sleep regulation uses additive competing drives,
not multiplicative suppression. Borbely (1982) Two-Process Model:

    Sleep onset when: S - C > threshold

Where S = sleep pressure (adenosine) and C = wake drive (circadian + energy + arousal).
Not: S × (1 − C).

**Fix:** Replaced multiplicative suppression with additive competitive model:
```python
        # Day 19.6 -- Borbely Two-Process Wake Drive (Sakurai 2007 orexin; Borbely 1982)
        # Competitive model: net_sleep_pressure = sleep_drive - wake_drive
        _wake_drive = max(0.0, (avg_energy - 0.5) * 0.8 + pp.error * 0.5 + da.level * 0.3)
        _wake_drive = min(0.10, _wake_drive)   # cap: gentle suppressor, preserves sleep cycling
        homeostasis.drives['sleep'] -= _wake_drive
        homeostasis.drives['sleep'] = max(0.0, homeostasis.drives['sleep'])
```

### 13.4 Bug 3 -- Foraging Floor Placed Before World Model Override

**Root cause:** The foraging floor ensuring baseline exploration was applied before the
`selected_action = _wm_best` world model override line. The world model subsequently
overwrote the foraging floor selection with its preferred action (almost always `withdraw`,
which has the lowest net cost at baseline), producing 0.000 explore rate across all waking
ticks despite the floor being in place.

**Action distribution diagnostic:** Over 19727 waking ticks: withdraw = 18529, approach =
1198, explore = 0. The world model's consistent preference for withdraw (cortisol -0.020,
energy +0.005, no learning cost) dominated without exception at baseline PE.

**Biological correction (user feedback):** Animals never stop exploring completely. Tonic
subcortical exploration drive (basal forebrain cholinergic system, lateral hypothalamus)
ensures baseline foraging regardless of cortical computation. This is foraging theory
(Stephens & Krebs 1986): organisms always maintain a minimum exploration rate.

**Fix:** Moved foraging floor to after the world model override:
```python
        # Day 19.6 -- Intrinsic foraging floor (Stephens & Krebs 1986 foraging theory)
        # Tonic subcortical exploration drive applied AFTER world model to prevent zero-explore attractor.
        # p_explore = 0.07 + curiosity ensures animals always maintain baseline foraging.
        if selected_action != 'SUPPRESSED' and selected_action != 'explore':
            _p_forage = max(0.07, homeostasis.drives.get('curiosity', 0.0) * 0.5)
            if random.random() < _p_forage:
                selected_action = 'explore'
```

## 17. Scientific Interpretation

### 17.1 Sleep as the Primary Biological Intelligence Mechanism

Across all experiments, the single most important factor in metabolic efficiency, behavioral
stability, and long-term survival was sleep. Experiment E demonstrated a 12.6x metabolic
advantage. Experiment A showed complete immunity to a catastrophic metabolic crisis during
sleep. Experiment I revealed the epistemic function of sleep (SHY reset). The sleep
architecture repair (section 13) restored the biological validity of the sleep cycle.

The key finding: **sleep is not a passive state but an active biological strategy**. The
Borbely two-process model correctly captures this -- sleep pressure is a competitive system,
not a passive accumulation. Process S (adenosine) and Process C (circadian wake drive)
compete additively, and the organism sleeps when sleep pressure exceeds wake drive by a
threshold -- a continuous biological bargaining process.

### 17.2 Metabolic Constraint as the Engine of Behavioral Diversity

The Intelligence Collapse Test (Experiment K) provided the clearest demonstration of a
principle implicit across all experiments: **metabolic constraint generates behavioral
diversity, not behavioural limitation**. Removing energy depletion (Mode B) eliminated the
approach behavioral mode entirely, reducing behavioral entropy from 0.95 to 0.41 bits.
Mode C (weak constraint) was even more degenerate (0.02 bits).

This has profound implications for AI architecture: systems with explicit resource budgets
that must be managed (compute, memory, bandwidth, battery) will develop richer, more adaptive
behavioral policies than unconstrained systems -- provided they have a world model capable
of selecting actions based on predicted resource trajectories.

### 17.3 Agency Requires World Model + Homeostasis

Experiment G (Resource Scarcity) demonstrated that neither the world model alone nor
homeostasis alone produces goal-directed agency. The BG without homeostatic bias signals
fails to select actions (L5 drives below threshold). The world model without hunger signals
defaults to a binary explore/withdraw policy. Together they produce the hunger-approach
correlation r = 0.89 -- the signature of genuine goal-directed behaviour (Dickinson 1985).
