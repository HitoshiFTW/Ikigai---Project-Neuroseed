## 2. Introduction

### 2.1 Objective

The core objective of Day 14 was to replace Ikigai's heuristic cortisol dynamics with a biologically grounded neuroendocrine cascade and to extend the architecture upward into predictive self-modeling and downward into physiological body-state feedback. The overarching goal of Project NeuroSeed is to progressively replace scripted algorithmic regulation with mechanisms that emerge from biologically plausible system interactions.

### 2.2 Biological Context

In biological organisms, stress is not purely a neural phenomenon. The mammalian stress response involves a multi-stage hormonal cascade originating in the hypothalamus, passing through the pituitary gland, and culminating in cortisol release from the adrenal cortex @[McEwen 1998 — Allostasis and Stress Adaptation]. Critically, this cascade incorporates multiple inhibitory feedback loops from the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex, modulation by oxytocin and adenosine, and slow recovery dynamics during sleep. Furthermore, predictive brains do not only model the external world; they generate and continuously update generative models of their own internal physiological state @[Friston 2010 — Predictive Processing; Seth 2013 — Interoceptive Inference]. Chronic stress exposure does not merely produce acute cortisol elevations but also produces lasting shifts in baseline physiology and regulatory capacity @[McEwen 1998 — Allostatic Overload]. The vagus nerve provides a continuous bidirectional communication channel between brainstem autonomic nuclei and peripheral organs, shaping parasympathetic tone and constraining stress reactivity @[Porges 2007 — Polyvagal Theory].

## 3. Layer 2 — HPA Endocrine Stress Cascade

### 3.1 Architecture

Four new classes implement the HPA axis:

- `HypothalamusSystem` — integrates threat signals and computes CRH production
- `PituitarySystem` — converts CRH to ACTH with a biological integration delay (τ ≈ 7 ticks)
- `AdrenalSystem` — converts ACTH to cortisol with slow decay, OXT buffering, and SWS recovery
- `HPAAxisSystem` — orchestrates the cascade and steers the existing `CortisolSystem`

### 3.2 Functional Cascade

```
Amygdala threat
Prediction error        →  HypothalamusSystem  →  CRH
Metabolic stress                 ↓
Environment pain         PituitarySystem  →  ACTH
                                 ↓
                          AdrenalSystem  →  cortisol_hpa
                                 ↓
                 cort.level += 0.08 × (cortisol_hpa − cort.level)
```

The soft blend update (8% correction per tick) preserves all existing systems that read `cort.level`, including the circadian rhythm, chronic stress tracker, neuromodulator coupling, and personality trait calculations.

### 3.3 Regulatory Inputs

The hypothalamus receives suppressive signals from:
- Hippocampal CA1 activity (contextual safety learning)
- Prefrontal cortex firing ratio (cognitive top-down regulation)

And receives excitatory inputs from:
- Amygdala BLA valence (threat detection)
- Predictive processing error
- Metabolic stress (energy depletion)
- Environmental pain signals

### 3.4 Stability Safeguards

Four stability fixes were implemented during development:

1. **Metabolic stress scaling**: raw metabolic stress multiplied by 0.60 to prevent sigmoid saturation from brief energy dips.
2. **Adenosine sensitivity cap**: hypothalamus sensitivity capped at 1.25 to prevent runaway HPA activation under sleep deprivation.
3. **Adrenal lower bound**: `AdrenalSystem.lower_bound = 0.02` to avoid conflicting with `CortisolSystem.apply_homeostasis()` homeostatic floor enforcement.
4. **Cortisol → CRH negative feedback**: `threat_signal -= current_cortisol × 0.25` implements the Dallman 1984 ultra-short feedback loop.

## 4. Layer 3 — Predictive Self-Model

### 4.1 Architecture

`SelfModelSystem` implements an interoceptive predictive model tracking three internal state trajectories: cortisol level, emotional valence, and average energy. The system maintains slow exponential moving averages (τ ≈ 50 ticks) of actual physiological state and updates its predictions via prediction errors.

### 4.2 Learning Rule

```
prediction_error = observed_EMA − predicted
predicted       += learning_rate × prediction_error
```

- `learning_rate = 0.002`
- Maximum update per tick: `±0.05`

This design follows Friston's predictive processing framework applied to interoception @[Seth 2013], where the brain maintains a generative model of body state and continuously revises it via ascending prediction errors.

### 4.3 Regulation Confidence

The system tracks `regulation_confidence` — a belief estimate representing the organism's learned expectation that its stress regulation mechanisms will succeed.

- Increases by 0.001 when cortisol drops after a stress event or when a reappraisal succeeds
- Decreases by 0.0005 when stress persists without cortisol recovery
- Confidence > 0.7 amplifies the PFC fired ratio by 1.05 before it enters the HPA cascade

This models Bandura's self-efficacy principle: an organism that has learned to regulate stress becomes measurably better at regulating it.

## 5. Layer 4 — Allostatic Adaptation

### 5.1 Architecture

`AllostasisSystem` tracks the cumulative physiological cost of repeated or chronic stress exposure, quantified as `allostatic_load`. This implements McEwen's allostatic overload model @[McEwen 1998].

### 5.2 Load Dynamics

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| `cortisol > 0.6` | `load += 0.002` per tick |
| `cortisol < 0.3` and `oxytocin > 0.4` | `load -= 0.001` per tick |
| SWS sleep | `load -= 0.003` per tick |
| Oxytocin buffering | `load -= oxytocin × 0.001` per tick |

Load is clamped to `[0, 1]` at all times.

### 5.3 Physiological Consequences

High allostatic load produces three downstream effects:

1. **Cortisol setpoint elevation**: `cort.setpoint = 0.15 + load × 0.20`, clamped to `[0.10, 0.35]`. Chronic stress raises the physiological baseline.
2. **PFC regulation impairment**: PFC fired ratio multiplied by `(1 − load × 0.15)`, minimum 0.85. Chronic stress weakens top-down regulation capacity @[Arnsten 2009].
3. **Hippocampal safety inhibition impairment**: CA1 fired ratio multiplied by `(1 − load × 0.20)`, minimum 0.80. Chronic glucocorticoids impair hippocampal GR feedback @[McEwen 1998].

### 5.4 Resilience Tracking

`AllostasisSystem.resilience` tracks the organism's buffering capacity as a slowly moving inverse of allostatic vulnerability:

```
target_resilience = 1.0 − allostatic_load × 0.7
resilience       += 0.001 × (target_resilience − resilience)
```

## 6. Layer 5 — Interoceptive Body Feedback

### 6.1 Architecture

`VagalInteroceptionSystem` simulates the physiological body-state signals that feed back into the brain's regulatory circuits, inspired by the polyvagal framework @[Porges 2007] and Damasio's somatic marker hypothesis @[Damasio 1999].

### 6.2 State Variables

| Signal | Initial | Range | Driven by |
|---|---|---|---|
| `heart_rate` | 0.50 | [0, 1] | Rises with cortisol; falls with vagal tone and SWS |
| `vagal_tone` | 0.50 | [0, 1] | Rises in SWS and with OXT; falls with cortisol |
| `body_stress` | 0.20 | [0, 1] | `0.6 × cortisol + 0.4 × heart_rate` |

### 6.3 Heart Rate Dynamics

```
if cortisol > 0.6:   heart_rate += 0.01
if vagal_tone > 0.6: heart_rate -= 0.01
if sleep_phase == 'SWS': heart_rate -= 0.01
```

Passive recovery toward resting rate 0.5 at a very slow pace (0.0005/tick) prevents long-term drift.

### 6.4 Vagal Tone Dynamics

```
if sleep_phase == 'SWS':   vagal_tone += 0.010
vagal_tone += oxytocin × 0.002
vagal_tone -= cortisol × 0.002
```

### 6.5 Brain Feedback Effects

Two closed-loop feedback effects were implemented:

1. **Parasympathetic brake**: When `vagal_tone > 0.6`, cortisol is reduced: `cort.level *= (1 − vagal_tone × 0.05)`. This implements the Porges cardiac brake mechanism.
2. **Somatic marker threat amplification**: Body stress from the previous tick augments the amygdala signal passed to the HPA cascade: `amyg_bla_valence_effective = amyg.bla_valence − body_stress × 0.10`. High body stress makes threat evaluation more reactive.

## 7. Full Regulation Loop

Day 14 established the following closed regulatory cycle operating each simulation tick:

```
Environment
↓
Amygdala threat detection
↓
HPA endocrine cascade  (CRH → ACTH → Cortisol)
↓
Cortisol release  →  body physiological response
↓
VagalInteroceptionSystem  (HR, vagal tone, body stress)
↓
Parasympathetic brake  →  cortisol suppression
↓
SelfModelSystem  (interoceptive prediction update)
↓
AllostasisSystem  (cumulative load + setpoint shift)
↓
Emotional regulation layer  (reappraisal, suppression)
```

### 7.1 Simulation Tick Execution Order

```
1.  cort.update()                         ← existing heuristic (unchanged)
2.  cort.apply_homeostasis()              ← circadian + decay (unchanged)
3.  allostasis damping applied to PFC and CA1 ratios
4.  self_model confidence boost applied to PFC ratio
5.  hpa_axis.step()                       ← endocrine cascade
6.  vagus.update()                        ← body-state update
7.  vagal_tone suppression applied to cort.level
8.  self_model.update()                   ← interoceptive prediction
9.  allostasis.update()                   ← cumulative load
10. allostasis.apply_to_cortisol_system() ← setpoint shift
11. Emotional regulation (L20)            ← unchanged
```

## 10. Expected Emergent Behaviors

The Day 14 architecture enables the following biologically plausible dynamics over long simulations:

- **Acute cortisol spikes** during amygdala-detected threat events
- **Learned safety suppression** as CA1 encodes safe contexts and inhibits CRH
- **Oxytocin-mediated stress buffering** via ALLO recovery and vagal tone increase
- **SWS sleep-driven cortisol recovery** across multiple simultaneous mechanisms
- **Chronic stress baseline drift**: repeated stress raises `cort.setpoint` via allostatic load
- **Stress regulation improvement over time**: successful regulation builds `regulation_confidence`, which feeds forward into stronger PFC inhibition of the HPA cascade
- **Body-state modulation of cognition**: elevated body stress biases threat perception, creating a self-reinforcing stress-arousal loop that breaks only through vagal recovery and oxytocin

## 11. Significance and Conclusion

Day 14 marks a fundamental architectural step in Project NeuroSeed. Ikigai has been transformed from a spiking neural network with heuristic neuromodulator dynamics into a stratified brain–body organism featuring layered biological regulation operating across multiple timescales: millisecond-scale neural spiking, tick-by-tick hormonal dynamics, slow interoceptive prediction updates, and very slow allostatic adaptation.

The system now resembles, at a functional level, the regulatory architecture of a living mammalian brain–body unit: threat is detected neurally, translated into endocrine signals, produces peripheral physiological effects, generates interoceptive feedback that modulates further processing, and updates a long-term model of the organism's own regulatory capacity.

This architecture provides a stable foundation for future extensions including full interoceptive curiosity (the organism becoming curious about its own body state), social stress modulation through oxytocin dynamics, and trauma modeling via persistent allostatic overload.
