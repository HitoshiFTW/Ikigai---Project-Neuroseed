Validation: **54/54 PASS**.

Phase C completed the first epoch's foundational triad by establishing the scientific hypothesis continuity layer. **Rule 2 (Frozen):** Aggression mapping: durations 1 and 3 route to `gentle_restart`; durations 7 and 14 route to `normal_restart`; durations 30 and 90 route to `blocker_first_aggressive`.

**Rule 3 (Frozen):** `RETENTION_FLOOR = 0.40` -- no milestone retention value may fall below 0.40 at any duration rung, categorically prohibiting identity collapse.

**Rule 4 (Frozen):** `HALF_LIFE_BONUS = +0.15` for hypotheses tagged as EEIL-primary, publication-coupled, or critically dependent.

**Rule 5 (Frozen):** Exactly one `UniversalHiatusPolicyKernel` output per tick -- the universal policy kernel uniqueness law.

**Rule 6 (Frozen):** `deque(maxlen=96)` on all eight classes.

`SilenceDurationClassifier` mapped any observed silence to its canonical ladder rung, computing `duration_confidence = clamp(kernel_confidence * 0.60 + reliability * 0.40)`. `TemporalContinuityScaler` read from a frozen scale table encoding `ms_cap`, `restart_depth`, `hyp_preservation`, `agenda_breadth`, and `strictness` for each rung, with `BASE_STRICTNESS = 0.75` and `scaling_delta = strictness - 0.75` as the primary downstream signal. `AdaptiveRestartAggression` converted the duration class to its frozen aggression mode via the Rule 2 mapping. `MilestoneRetentionCurve` applied the Rule 3 floor; the full retention table was `{1: 1.00, 3: 0.95, 7: 0.85, 14: 0.75, 30: 0.60, 90: 0.45}`, each value clamped above 0.40. `HypothesisHalfLifeModel` computed `survival_pressure = clamp(n_survive/n_total + bonus)` with base half-lives `{1:30, 3:25, 7:20, 14:15, 30:10, 90:7}` days. `AgendaBreadthRegulator` set the maximum active agenda items permitted at each rung: 5 for micro and short breaks, 4 for week and long breaks, 3 for semester and seasonal silences. `LongitudinalIdentityStabilizer` computed `identity_stability = clamp(persona_stability * 0.40 + retention_floor * 0.30 + kernel_confidence * 0.30)`, classifying risk as low (>= 0.70), moderate (>= 0.50), or high (< 0.50). `UniversalHiatusPolicyKernel` aggregated all seven upstream classes into one canonical multi-timescale policy state carrying the frozen duration ladder, retention floor, aggression mapping, half-life doctrine, agenda breadth law, and an `identity_floor = max(0.50, identity_stability)`. ## IX. Interactive Conversational Agency -- Phase M

Phase M introduced the first conversational agency layer in NeuroSeed history, enabling the organism to engage in adaptive multi-turn reasoning partnerships where goals evolve, ambiguities are repaired, revisions accumulate, and plans emerge from principled negotiation. The theoretical foundation synthesises Clark and Schaefer's (1989) contribution model, Traum's (1994) dialogue move taxonomy, and Grosz and Sidner's (1986) discourse structure theory.

Five frozen constants govern Phase M.

**Rule 1 (Frozen):** `CLARIFICATION_CAP = 3` -- maximum clarification questions per turn, preventing recursive clarification loops.

**Rule 2 (Frozen):** `AMBIGUITY_THRESHOLD = 0.50` -- any output with `ambiguity_score = clamp(intent_drift * 0.60 + (1 - lang_conf) * 0.40) >= 0.50` must route through `ClarificationQuestionPlanner` before response finalisation.

**Rule 3 (Frozen):** `REVISION_FLOOR = 2` -- at least two prior revisions must remain represented in the collaborative revision loop, preventing isolated one-shot outputs.

**Rule 4 (Frozen):** `NEGOTIATION_BONUS = +0.15` for collaborative plan steps touching `milestone_blocker`, `code_patch`, `experiment_launch`, `paper_claim`, or `project_pivot`.

**Rule 5 (Frozen):** Exactly one `ConversationalAgencyKernel` output per tick.

`IntentEvolutionTracker` monitored five canonical intent slots (original_goal, revised_goal_1, emergent_task_shift, clarified_blocker, changed_priority) with per-slot stability scores and computed `intent_drift = clamp(initial_stability - terminal_stability)` as the primary ambiguity estimate input. `ClarificationQuestionPlanner` enforced Rules 1 and 2: questions were drawn from a priority-sorted seed list and capped at three; when `ambiguity_score >= 0.50`, all generated questions carried `routed_via_ambiguity = True`. `CollaborativeRevisionLoop` maintained five revision seeds (baseline_plan, revision_1, revision_2, tentative_edit_1, constraint_update) and enforced Rule 3 by promoting pending revisions to accepted status if `n_accepted` fell below 2. `GoalNegotiationEngine` maintained five negotiation axes -- user_goal_vs_milestone, patch_vs_doctrine, research_vs_product, experiment_vs_paper, explanation_vs_execution -- all carrying at least one bonus tag, so all five axes received `NEGOTIATION_BONUS = +0.15`. Negotiation confidence was the mean of the five axis scores, and `dominant_axis = argmax(axis_conf)`. `AmbiguityResolutionBridge` mirrored Rule 2: when `clar_routed = True`, all five resolution items (wording, scope, timeline, domain, experimental) were marked `clar_assisted = True`. `TurnLevelMemoryBinder` consolidated five binding slots and preserved `n_accepted_revisions` and `prior_turns` for downstream use. `PartneredPlanningSynthesizer` constructed six-step collaborative plans (PLAN_STEP_CAP = 6), applying `NEGOTIATION_BONUS = +0.15` to all six steps and carrying `dominant_axis` from `GoalNegotiationEngine` into the plan structure. `ConversationalAgencyKernel` aggregated via `kernel_confidence = clamp(neg_conf * 0.20 + plan_conf * 0.20 + rev_coh * 0.15 + amb_conf * 0.15 + lang_conf * 0.15 + bind_conf * 0.15)` and carried all five mandatory priors, the `kernel_uniqueness` sentinel, negotiated goal axis, clarification path, revision loop state, and full collaboration rationale.
