## 1. Objective

The primary objective of Day 12 was to introduce a **structured cortical processing architecture** directly into the core simulation loop. This upgrade organizes neural computation into layered biological pathways instead of relying on flat, undifferentiated population flows. 

The goal was to achieve:
* A biologically plausible cortical hierarchy
* Predictive processing capabilities
* Structured feedforward and feedback learning mechanisms
* Stable neural competition mediated through dedicated local inhibition

This architectural leap marks the definitive transition of Ikigai from a **generic neural network to a proto-cortical brain architecture**.

---

## 2. Architecture Introduced

A new **CorticalColumn system** was implemented. The cortex is now explicitly composed of distinct, functionally specialized, layered neuron populations forming the first proto-cortical processing column inside Ikigai.

### Layer 4 (L4)
* **Role:** Sensory Input Layer
* **Scale:** ~30 neurons
* **Function:** Receives high-fidelity parallel raw environment vectors. It features minimal intra-layer recurrence, functioning primarily as a fast relay.

### Layer 2/3 (L23)
* **Role:** Associative Cortex
* **Scale:** ~60 neurons
* **Function:** Features dense recurrent attractor networks (~15% local connectivity). This layer is responsible for generating internal representations, maintaining working memory, driving the prediction system, and interfacing bidirectionally with the hippocampus.

### Layer 5 (L5)
* **Role:** Motor/Output Cortex
* **Scale:** ~30 neurons
* **Function:** Receives processed and filtered activity from L2/3, converting those representations into concrete, competing behavioral action commands.

### Local Inhibitory Pool
* **Scale:** ~15 interneurons
* **Function:** Operates as a shared inhibitory microcircuit tracking L4, L2/3, and L5. It enforces immediate biologically realistic winner-take-all (WTA) competition, sharpening the neural coding representations before they propagate.

---

## 3. Predictive Processing System

A rigorous predictive coding mechanism was integrated directly into the structural loop. 

L2/3 acts as a predictive engine continuously attempting to predict the incoming sensory state of L4. This prediction is generated mathematically through an explicit **prediction matrix (60 × 30)**.

The prediction error is strictly computed as:
`error = actual_L4 − predicted_L4`

Crucially, **L4 activity is never modified or biased by this prediction.** The generated prediction error only serves to update the prediction weights in the matrix. 

This error-driven learning is actively modulated by physiological state:
* **Dopamine** scales the learning rate (representing a reward/salience signal).
* **Norepinephrine** scales the error precision.

---

## 4. Cortical Processing Order

A strict, biologically plausible processing pipeline was implemented inside the central simulation tick loop. This sequential ordering ensures that inhibition cannot prematurely suppress the formation of cortical representations.

1. **Step 1:** The Environment generates its sensory vector.
2. **Step 2:** L4 neurons receive sensory input and spike accordingly.
3. **Step 3:** L4 spikes propagate forward into L2/3.
4. **Step 4:** L2/3 performs recurrent attractor settling across 3 internal cycles, allowing representations to stabilize.
5. **Step 5:** The local inhibitory microcircuit evaluates the active neurons and enforces winner-take-all competition.
6. **Step 6:** The prediction system computes the precision-weighted prediction error between L2/3 and the sensory truth in L4.
7. **Step 7:** Hippocampal interaction occurs (either STDP encoding from L2/3 into CA3, or pattern completion retrieval from CA1 back into L2/3).
8. **Step 8:** L2/3 propagates the heavily processed signals down to L5 motor neurons.
9. **Step 9:** Motor output triggers behavioral action if thresholds are reached.

## 5. Oscillatory Modulation

We successfully integrated continuous global brain rhythms across the entire cellular population.

* **Theta Rhythm:** ~4-8 Hz analogue equivalent, oscillating with an amplitude of `±0.08`.
* **Gamma Rhythm:** ~40 Hz analogue equivalent, oscillating with an amplitude of `±0.04`.

These oscillations globally modulate the baseline neuronal firing thresholds. This dynamic phase-shifting coordinates spike timing, encouraging neurons to fire in rhythmic, unified clusters without inducing unnatural or catastrophic synchronized bursting (seizures).

## 6. Integration with Existing Systems

The Proto-Cortex connects flawlessly with existing legacy brain modules:

* **Hippocampus:** L2/3 projects to CA3 for rapid episodic encoding, while CA1 projects back to L2/3 to drive pattern completion during memory retrieval.
* **Neuromodulators:** Dopamine explicitly reinforces learning rates, and Norepinephrine manages prediction error precision.
* **Sleep System:** Sharp-Wave Ripple (SWR) replay sequences organically continue to operate, now utilizing the rich cortical activity traces left by the associative column.
* **EI Balance:** The global EI homeostatic tracker remains actively running, monitoring total systemic excitatory vs inhibitory stability as a slow safety boundary.
