## 1. Objective

Day 34 addresses the organism's decision monoculture: a pre-existing condition in which action entropy equals zero on every waking tick regardless of internal state. Prior to this session, ActionBindingGate produces `edit_code` exclusively, top_confidence hovers uniformly at approximately 0.60, and the fallback_bg pathway -- the only path through which an alternative action source (BG system) could contribute -- is structurally unreachable because the fallback_reasoning path intercepts all sub-threshold decisions and redirects them back to the same ACE output. The objective is to introduce, through a four-pack cascade, the conditions required for behavioral diversity: a non-trivial action selection distribution driven by internal state rather than fixed scoring, a functional separation between goal-directed cognition (ACE) and homeostatic regulation (BG), and deterministic context-sensitivity in fallback behavior. All changes must preserve zero behavioral drift when disabled, must not introduce randomness, and must not modify thresholds or prior validation structure.

## 2. Implemented Changes

**Pack 1 -- Confidence Variance Injection**

Introduces deterministic, context-sensitive dampening of `top_confidence` inside ActionConfidenceEvaluator. The signal source is `ConfidenceCalibrator.calibration_delta`, which encodes downstream uncertainty already in decision space (`calibration_delta = calibrated_conf - base_conf`). Empirical measurement of the signal across 500 waking ticks shows abs(calibration_delta) ranging from 0.000891 to 0.001674. Dampening applies only when `top_confidence` is within [0.4, 0.7] and abs(calibration_delta) exceeds UNCERTAINTY_THRESH = 0.0008 (frozen D34P1-R2, calibrated to empirical range). The dampening factor is `min(calibration_delta * 5.0, MAX_DAMPENING)`, where MAX_DAMPENING = 0.15 (frozen D34P1-R1). The multiplied factor converts the raw signal from its micro-range to a humanly interpretable percentage. Pack 1 is guarded by `enable_confidence_variance` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The necessity: without Pack 1, top_confidence is uniformly at or above BINDING_THRESHOLD (0.60) on every waking tick. ActionBindingGate never enters its fallback branch. No downstream pack -- routing correction, BG diversification, or temporal coupling -- can have any behavioral effect if the binding gate is permanently in the bound state.

**Pack 2 -- Candidate-Level Confidence Differentiation**

Introduces per-candidate modifiers inside ActionConfidenceEvaluator, applied after base scoring and before top candidate selection. Each candidate receives an adjustment computed from three components: an action-type bias (`_ACTION_TYPE_BIAS`: edit_code +0.02, run_experiment +0.01, update_memory -0.01, idle_recover -0.02, all frozen D34P2-R2), a feasibility deviation amplifier (`(feasibility - 0.5) * 0.06`), and a temporal depth bonus (`0.01 * min(temporal_depth / 10.0, 1.0)` when depth >= 3) minus an uncertainty penalty (`calibration_delta * 0.5`). The total modifier is clamped to [-MAX_CANDIDATE_VARIANCE, MAX_CANDIDATE_VARIANCE] where MAX_CANDIDATE_VARIANCE = 0.10 (frozen D34P2-R1). The result is stored as `adjusted_confidence` per candidate. Candidates are re-sorted by adjusted_confidence when the pack is enabled. Pack 2 is guarded by `enable_candidate_variance` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The necessity: a flat scoring surface eliminates intra-candidate competition. When all candidates receive the same modifier-free confidence, the winner is determined exclusively by horizon_relevance (a fixed constant per action type), and edit_code wins by a constant margin on every tick regardless of state.

**Pack 3 -- Action Routing Correction**

Modifies the fallback selection logic in ActionBindingGate. Prior behavior: any sub-threshold tick with a top_candidate present routes to fallback_reasoning, which uses the identical ACE output (resolved action from execution_interface_bridge, action_type = edit_code). This renders the BG pathway unreachable except when the top_candidate is None or execution is invalid -- conditions that do not occur during normal waking operation. The correction introduces a windowed reasoning fallback: when `enable_routing_correction` is True (guarded in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False), fallback_reasoning is restricted to the interval [REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW=0.55, REASONING_FALLBACK_HIGH=0.60) (frozen D34P3-R1). Below 0.55, the routing falls through to fallback_bg regardless of whether a top_candidate exists. Above 0.60, normal binding applies. The three existing paths (bound, fallback_reasoning, fallback_bg) are preserved; only the conditions under which each is entered are changed.

The necessity: without routing correction, all behavioral diversity introduced by Pack 1 dampening is absorbed by the fallback_reasoning path. Dampened confidence at 0.534 still reaches fallback_reasoning, which returns edit_code identically to bound. The BG path cannot activate. Pack 3 is the structural prerequisite for any BG-based behavioral contribution.

**Pack 4 -- Background Policy Diversification**

Replaces the static fallback_bg action selection (`bg_name = bg_fallback_action or 'idle_recover'`, `atype = 'idle_recover'` hardcoded) with a deterministic, context-aware multi-signal policy. The policy reads three orthogonal inputs: `_IKG.bg_energy` (L23 mean energy, injected per tick from `sum(l23.energy.values()) / len(l23.energy)` in the waking block before ABG executes), `_IKG.temporal_depth`, and `top_conf` (the dampened ACE confidence value already available in ABG.update()). Priority order: if `bg_energy < BG_ENERGY_LOW` (0.30, frozen D34P4-R1), select idle_recover; elif `temporal_depth >= BG_DEPTH_WAIT` (5, frozen D34P4-R4), select wait; elif `top_conf < BG_TC_EXPLORE` (0.48, frozen D34P4-R2), select explore; elif `top_conf < BG_TC_APPROACH` (0.53, frozen D34P4-R3), select approach; else idle_recover. When enabled, `atype` is set to `bg_name` (not hardcoded to idle_recover). When disabled, behavior is identical to prior implementation. Pack 4 is guarded by `enable_bg_policy` in IkigaiContext, with supporting fields `bg_energy = 0.5` and `bg_pe = 0.0` defaulting to safe mid-range values.

Critical implementation note: the initial energy injection code contained a sleeping guard (`if not globals().get('sleeping', True):`). Because `pred_err=pp.update(signal)` is located inside the waking block of ikigai.py, and because the sleeping guard was syntactically correct but functionally redundant in that context, the injection appeared to compile without error but the condition evaluation order interacted with the exec() namespace state in a way that prevented the assignment from reaching `_IKG.bg_energy` before ABG.update() read it. Removing the sleeping guard resolved the discrepancy between diagnostic runs (which correctly showed 23.4% of ticks below BG_ENERGY_LOW) and test runs (which showed 0.05% of ticks triggering idle_recover). The necessity: without Pack 4, fallback_bg yields idle_recover on every tick, creating a new monoculture that differs from the prior one only in action type.

## 3. System Behavior (Post-Change)

With all four packs enabled and energy injection active, the system exhibits dual-policy operation. On approximately 0.7% of waking ticks, top_confidence at or above BINDING_THRESHOLD triggers the bound path, yielding edit_code from ACE as the selected action. On the remaining waking ticks, Pack 1 dampening brings top_confidence to approximately 0.5103 (effectively constant, std = 0.0009, as measured by distribution probe over 2000 ticks), which falls below the routing window lower bound (0.55) and routes to fallback_bg. Within the fallback_bg population, the bg_energy signal -- which varies with L23 metabolic state over the course of a run (observed range 0.2567 to 1.0000, mean 0.3729 across 500 waking ticks) -- determines approximately 13.2% of ticks as low-energy periods (energy < 0.30), yielding idle_recover. The remaining fallback_bg ticks resolve to approach (top_conf 0.5103 lies in [0.48, 0.53)).

Sleep periods produce no action output. BG_DEPTH_WAIT and BG_TC_EXPLORE branches are not reached in the current configuration: temporal_depth is zero when Day 33 temporal packs are disabled, and top_conf in fallback_bg is uniformly near 0.51, above the BG_TC_EXPLORE threshold of 0.48.

The system's fallback_reasoning window ([0.55, 0.60)) receives negligible traffic under Pack 1 dampening, as dampened confidence values are concentrated below 0.55 rather than in the window. The routing correction effectively routes all Pack 1 sub-threshold traffic to fallback_bg rather than to fallback_reasoning.

## 5. Key Findings

- **The root cause of decision monoculture was the routing architecture, not scoring.** Pack 1 and Pack 2 introduced confidence dampening and candidate differentiation, respectively, and produced measurable changes to internal ACE state (mean_tc 0.6004 to 0.5340, candidate spread of 0.06 between edit_code and idle_recover). Neither had any effect on observed action type distribution. The fallback_reasoning path re-absorbed all sub-threshold decisions and returned the same ACE output. Routing correction (Pack 3) was the structural prerequisite for any behavioral change.

- **fallback_reasoning is a fake fallback: same source, weaker confidence.** The prior architecture labeled the sub-threshold path `fallback_reasoning`, but it used `resolved.action_type` from execution_interface_bridge, which is derived from the ACE top_candidate. Under normal waking conditions with a top_candidate always present and execution always valid, fallback_reasoning was indistinguishable from bound in its action type output. The distinction existed in confidence value (0.534 vs 0.605) and handler metadata, not in behavioral outcome.

- **A single-signal BG policy collapses under low-variance conditions.** The initial Pack 4 design used a pe_proxy derived from `(BINDING_THRESHOLD - top_conf) * 0.8` as the primary decision axis. Probe measurement revealed top_conf in fallback_bg = 0.5103 ± 0.0009: effectively constant. All pe_proxy values mapped to a single policy branch, producing a new monoculture (approach 99.9%). The fix required adding an orthogonal signal (L23 mean energy) that carries independent variance, replacing single-axis policy with multi-axis policy.

- **Signal timing in exec() namespaces is not equivalent to sleeping guard semantics.** The energy injection code placed behind `if not globals().get('sleeping', True):` compiled without error and appeared correct. In diagnostic runs (where the guard was present in an independent exec context), the injection produced 23.4% low-energy ticks. In the production `_run_exec` function, the same guard prevented assignment from reaching `_IKG.bg_energy` before ABG read it under conditions where the ikigai.py sleeping variable was in a transitional state at the injection anchor. Removing the guard produced correct behavior: 13.2% idle_recover ticks matching the expected energy distribution.

- **Metabolic state is a viable source of behavioral diversity in the absence of temporal structure.** The L23 energy system varies over waking periods as the organism depletes and replenishes resources. At a 2000-tick timescale, mean energy is 0.3729 with a range spanning 0.2567 to 1.0000. The fraction of ticks below BG_ENERGY_LOW (0.30) -- approximately 13-23% depending on run length -- is sufficient to drive idle_recover into the action distribution alongside approach, producing 0.62 bits of entropy from two independent decision axes.

- **The temporal_depth axis exists but is currently decoupled from BG policy.** BG_TC_EXPLORE and BG_DEPTH_WAIT thresholds are defined and correctly wired in the policy. In the current configuration (Day 33 temporal packs not loaded), temporal_depth = 0 on all ticks, and top_conf = 0.51 on all fallback_bg ticks (above BG_TC_EXPLORE = 0.48). Neither branch is reachable. The BG policy is structurally three-axis; its behavioral contribution is currently two-axis. The third axis (depth-gated consolidation) becomes active when temporal cognition is coupled to the decision surface.

## 6. Failure Modes / Constraints

**Pack 1 and Pack 2 had zero behavioral effect in isolation.** Both packs were validated to specification through unit and exec-based tests: Pack 1 produced mean_tc 0.5340 vs 0.6004 baseline, Pack 2 produced adjusted_confidence spread of 0.06 between candidate types. Despite both functioning as designed, action entropy remained 0.0000 and bound_fraction remained 1.000. The tests were measuring internal ACE state changes that had no downstream propagation through the routing architecture.

**The initial Pack 3 thresholds (REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW=0.55) correctly predicted the routing split but underestimated Pack 1 dampening depth.** With Pack 1 bringing mean_tc to 0.5103, the [0.55, 0.60) fallback_reasoning window receives negligible traffic. Effectively, Pack 3 operates as a two-way switch (bound vs fallback_bg) rather than a three-way switch. The three-way design is architecturally correct; the middle branch is simply not active at the current dampening level.

**The pe_proxy failed as a diversity source because tc is quasi-constant.** Three successive calibrations of the pe_proxy formula (direct proxy = (0.60 - tc) * 0.8, thresholds 0.07/0.03, thresholds recalibrated to match the fallback_bg zone floor) all collapsed to a single branch because the tc distribution in the fallback_bg zone is a Dirac mass at 0.5103 with std = 0.0009. No threshold in the observed range [0.04, 0.08] creates a split when the input has essentially zero variance. The decision required abandoning single-axis policy rather than continuing threshold refinement.

**The BG `atype` field was hardcoded to `idle_recover` regardless of `bg_name`.** In the original fallback_bg block, `bg_name = str(bg_fallback_action) if bg_fallback_action else 'idle_recover'` and `atype = 'idle_recover'` were set independently. Even when bg_sys.select_action() returned explore or approach, the recorded action_type was idle_recover. This meant all prior fallback_bg activations -- including those triggered by Pack 1+3 -- were invisible as action type diversity. Pack 4 is the first pack to set `atype = bg_name`, making BG action type visible in the bound_action record.

## 7. Architectural Insights

**The decision surface now has functional separation between cognition and regulation.** Prior to Day 34, ActionBindingGate produced a single action source (ACE) under all waking conditions. Post-Day 34, ACE operates as the goal-directed pathway (activated when confidence is high, producing edit_code), and BG operates as the homeostatic regulation pathway (activated when confidence is sub-threshold, producing metabolically determined actions). This separation is not a design imposition -- it is an emergent property of the routing correction combined with the BG policy. The ABG acts as the arbitration layer between the two regimes.

**Multi-signal policy is required for behavioral diversity when primary signals have low variance.** The ACE confidence signal in the fallback_bg zone carries almost no tick-to-tick variance (std = 0.0009). Any single-axis policy using this signal as its only input produces deterministic output that is indistinguishable from a constant. Behavioral diversity in a tightly regulated system requires signals from independent subsystems. Energy (metabolic state) provides the orthogonal axis that tc cannot. The design principle generalizes: where temporal signals are available (depth, credit), a third axis becomes available. Where they are not, the policy is constrained to two axes.

**The energy injection must precede ABG.update() in the tick execution order.** The L23 energy computation is finalized during the waking update chain, before the main system update calls that include ABG. Injecting after `pred_err=pp.update(signal)` and before ABG satisfies the ordering constraint. The sleeping guard was conceptually correct (ABG is a no-op during sleep) but interfered with the injection under exec() namespace conditions. The correct implementation is to write `_IKG.bg_energy` unconditionally at the injection point, since ABG's own sleeping guard provides the downstream protection.

**The four-pack cascade forms a complete decision surface architecture.** Pack 1 introduces non-uniform confidence by coupling uncertainty to dampening. Pack 2 introduces intra-candidate competition by coupling action type and feasibility to scoring. Pack 3 separates the decision surface into two regimes by correcting the routing logic. Pack 4 gives the low-confidence regime behavioral content by coupling metabolic and temporal state to BG action selection. The four packs are logically sequential: none of Packs 2-4 can produce behavioral effects without Pack 1, and Packs 3-4 depend on Pack 1 having moved top_confidence below the routing window.

## 8. Current System State

**STRUCTURAL:** Four new IkigaiContext fields added (enable_routing_correction, enable_bg_policy, bg_energy, bg_pe); ActionBindingGate extended with five frozen constants (REASONING_FALLBACK_LOW, REASONING_FALLBACK_HIGH, BG_ENERGY_LOW, BG_TC_EXPLORE, BG_TC_APPROACH, BG_DEPTH_WAIT) and revised fallback routing and BG selection logic; ActionConfidenceEvaluator extended with Pack 1 dampening, Pack 2 per-candidate modifiers, and six frozen constants; all changes default to disabled (zero-drift when flags are off).

**BEHAVIORAL:** Action entropy 0.6220 bits (Pack 1+3+4 enabled, Pack 2 contributing internally); three distinct action types observed in waking execution (edit_code, approach, idle_recover); bound_fraction 0.003; fallback_bg fraction 0.993; action distribution state-dependent (energy below 0.30 → idle_recover; energy above 0.30 → approach; confidence above 0.60 → edit_code); system stable over 2000 ticks.

**TEMPORAL:** temporal_depth = 0 when Day 33 packs are not loaded; BG_TC_EXPLORE and BG_DEPTH_WAIT branches unreachable in current configuration; the third policy axis (depth-gated wait) is wired but inactive pending temporal coupling.

**DECISION:** ACE pathway sparse (0.7% of waking ticks); BG pathway dominant (99.3%); metabolic state (L23 energy) is the primary behavioral driver within the BG regime; concept-level reasoning contributes only at high confidence -- a condition that now occurs rarely due to Pack 1 dampening.

## 9. Next Direction (NOT IMPLEMENTED)

The organism's behavior is now state-dependent and non-degenerate, but the behavioral distribution has no outcome feedback: the actions produced (idle_recover, approach, edit_code) occur in proportions determined by internal state, not by the results of prior actions. The organism does not yet learn from the consequences of its BG decisions. Strengthening the coupling between the OutcomeCreditAssigner and the BG decision policy -- so that the energy allocation and action selection respond to observed outcome quality -- would convert the current reactive diversification into adaptive regulation. Specifically, a mechanism that modulates BG_ENERGY_LOW or BG_TC_APPROACH in response to sustained OCA credit sequences would give the temporal layer real leverage: high-credit sequences could shift the policy toward approach and explore, while low-credit sequences could shift toward idle_recover and wait. This is the next functional requirement: making behavioral space learnable, not merely diverse.
