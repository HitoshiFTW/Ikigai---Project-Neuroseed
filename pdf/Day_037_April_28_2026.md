## 1. Objective

Day 36 established the full credit and execution architecture: ACS is action-stratified (std = 0.02282), exec_diversity maps BG primitives to semantic execution types (approach -> edit_code, explore -> run_experiment), and the BG scoring block uses equalized 0.45 bases with credit as the sole differentiating signal. The final Day 36 result is run_experiment = 0.0% across all run lengths. The structural cause is a persistent credit gap: at ACS mean = 0.326 (credit_n = 0.663), approach scores 0.649 and explore scores 0.551 -- a gap of 0.098. PE pressure (K_PE = 0.18, PE mean = 0.009) contributes at most 0.032 to the explore side at peak observed PE. Explore cannot win the scoring block under observed signal ranges. The Day 36 analysis identifies two candidate paths forward: reducing credit pressure (risking loss of the credit-to-behavior causal chain established by Pack 5) or introducing an independent signal that makes unexplored state regions transiently attractive without modifying the existing credit architecture.

Day 37 takes the second path. The four-pack program is: (1) novelty pressure -- a visit-count-based score injection that boosts explore and suppresses approach in states with low visit history; (2) calibration -- coefficient tuning to bring run_experiment into a meaningful but not dominant range; (3) contextual curiosity -- gate the novelty signal so exploration fires only when BOTH novelty AND prediction-credit mismatch are high simultaneously; (4) curiosity reinforcement -- remember which state contexts have historically produced rewarding exploration and apply that memory as a learned score bonus. All four packs operate exclusively inside the BG scoring block, default to disabled (zero behavioral drift when all flags are False), and do not modify top_confidence, ACS, credit_n, PE signal, or any routing threshold.

## 2. Implemented Changes

**Pack 1 -- Novelty Pressure**

Introduces a visit-count-based exploration signal into the BG scoring block. A coarse state key is computed each tick from three discretized signals: `(last_action_type, int(bg_energy * 5), int(bg_pe * 10))`. The energy bucket provides five quantization levels over [0.0, 1.0]; the PE bucket provides ten levels over [0.0, 1.0]; last_action_type is the raw BG primitive from the previous tick (not the exec_diversity-remapped type). This produces a state fingerprint that changes when energy regime, PE regime, or recent action changes. The novelty score for the current key is `nov_n = 1.0 / (1.0 + math.log(1.0 + count))`, where count is the number of visits stored in `_IKG.novelty_counts[key]`. At count = 0, nov_n = 1.0; at count = 1, nov_n = 0.590; the function is monotonically decreasing and bounded [0, 1]. Pack 1 is guarded by `enable_novelty` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The injection applies inside the BG scoring block, after Pack 6 PE pressure and before the argmax: `_sc_explore += K_N * nov_n`; `_sc_approach -= 0.3 * K_N * nov_n`; both clamped to >= 0.0. The count for the current key is incremented by 1.0 AFTER the action is committed (after exec_diversity, after last_action_type is updated), ensuring the count reflects visits that have already occurred rather than the current selection. An initial injection formula, `_sc_approach -= 0.5 * K_N * (1 - nov_n)`, was rejected immediately: this formulation penalizes approach when `(1 - nov_n)` is large, which is when count is HIGH, which is when the state is FAMILIAR. A mechanism that suppresses approach in familiar states amplifies approach in novel states -- the reverse of the intended effect. The correct direction is suppression proportional to nov_n (novel states), which pushes the system toward explore exactly when the state has low visit history. The 0.3 coefficient on approach suppression is intentionally smaller than the 1.0 coefficient on explore boost, preventing bidirectional score collapse.

A critical implementation gap discovered during Pack 1 validation: the novelty_counts dictionary accumulates without bound. With 13 unique state keys under typical operating conditions, all keys saturate within 100 ticks. At count = 100, nov_n = 1/(1 + log(101)) = 0.218; at count = 500, nov_n = 0.160. The entire novelty signal collapses to a near-constant low-amplitude bias, indistinguishable from baseline noise. The fix is count decay: all values in novelty_counts are multiplied by 0.995 every tick, applied in a loop over all keys AFTER the count increment. This produces an equilibrium at which the decay rate equals the visit rate: for a key visited on every tick, count_eq = 1 / (1 - 0.995) = 200 (nov_n_eq = 0.159). For a key visited at rate 1/13 (one of 13 equally likely states), count_eq = (1/13) / 0.005 = 15.4 (nov_n_eq = 0.263). At equilibrium, nov_n is bounded well away from zero, sustaining a non-trivial novelty signal indefinitely. Initial K_N = 0.12.

**Pack 2 -- Novelty Coefficient Calibration**

Pack 1 with K_N = 0.12 produces run_experiment = 0.6% at 2000 ticks -- well below the target range of 3-10%. The scoring arithmetic reveals the constraint: at ACS mean (credit_n = 0.658), approach scores 0.647 and explore scores 0.553, giving a gap of 0.094. For explore to win, the novelty injection must overcome this gap: `K_N * nov_n + 0.3 * K_N * nov_n >= 0.094`, i.e., `1.3 * K_N * nov_n >= 0.094`. At K_N = 0.12, the winning condition requires nov_n > 0.603, corresponding to count < 0.93. This threshold is so tight that explore wins only on the very first visit to a state before any count has accumulated, a window of approximately one tick per state transition. The result is an extremely low explore rate that matches the observed 0.6%.

Pack 2 changes a single parameter: K_N from 0.12 to 0.20. At K_N = 0.20, the winning condition requires nov_n > 0.362, corresponding to count < approximately 5. At the typical visit rate (1/13), count reaches 5 after roughly 70 ticks of continuous visits; after 70 ticks without visiting, count decays back below 5 in roughly 130 ticks. The effective explore cycle length drops from the near-zero window of K_N = 0.12 to a window that opens and closes on a timescale of tens to low hundreds of ticks, producing a sustained explore rate of approximately 5.1% at 2000 ticks. No other parameter is modified; no new IkigaiContext fields are added. The test harness for Pack 2 is the Pack 1 harness re-run with K_N = 0.20.

**Pack 3 -- Contextual Curiosity**

Pack 1 and Pack 2 establish novelty as a scoring signal but the signal fires uniformly: any state with low visit count triggers the explore boost, regardless of what the organism is experiencing. Contextual curiosity conditions the novelty boost on the simultaneous presence of both novelty and prediction-credit mismatch. Mismatch is defined as the degree to which the current PE level is inconsistent with the current credit level: high PE and high credit_n is a genuine mismatch (the organism is performing well by credit signal but experiencing high prediction error -- a situation that rewards investigation); low PE and low credit_n is the converse. The curiosity signal is `curiosity = nov_n * mismatch`, which is zero when either novelty is absent (nov_n = 0) or mismatch is absent (mismatch = 0), and maximal only when both are simultaneously present. Pack 3 is guarded by `enable_contextual_curiosity` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The mismatch formula required three iterations. The first version used `mismatch = abs(pe - (1.0 - credit_n))`. With PE mean = 0.009 and credit_n = 0.658, this evaluates to abs(0.009 - 0.342) = 0.333 on nearly every tick: a near-constant bias that provides no contextual discrimination. The second version used `mismatch = pe * (1.0 - credit_n)`. With PE = 0.009 and (1 - credit_n) = 0.342, this produces mismatch = 0.003 on average -- effectively dormant. The reason is that raw PE is not on the same scale as (1 - credit_n): PE operating in a range of 0.005-0.015 while credit_n operates in a range of 0.62-0.70 produces a product three orders of magnitude smaller than the credit component. The third and final version normalizes PE to its practical range before multiplication: `pe_n = min(1.0, pe / 0.10); mismatch = pe_n * (1.0 - credit_n)`. With PE at its mean of 0.009, pe_n = 0.09, giving mismatch = 0.09 * 0.342 = 0.031; at the 95th percentile of PE (~0.028), mismatch = 0.28 * 0.342 = 0.096. The resulting distribution is dynamic: mean = 0.032, std = 0.039, max = 0.354, with occasional high-mismatch ticks that drive burst-like explore selection events. K_CTX = 0.15. The scoring injection parallels Pack 1: `_sc_explore += K_CTX * curiosity`; `_sc_approach -= 0.25 * K_CTX * curiosity`; clamped >= 0.0.

The burst analysis (V5b) quantifies the temporal structure introduced by Pack 3. Because mismatch is low on typical ticks and elevated only during PE excursions, the curiosity signal is episodic. When novelty and mismatch both peak simultaneously, explore wins several consecutive ticks, forming a burst. The inter-burst gap is determined by the time for PE to return to baseline and for mismatch to fall, and by the time for the state key count to re-accumulate after the burst visits. This structure -- short dense runs separated by approach-dominant gaps -- is qualitatively different from the sparse single-tick explore events produced by Pack 1 and Pack 2 alone.

**Pack 4 -- Curiosity Reinforcement**

Packs 1-3 make exploration situationally attractive but retain no memory of whether past exploration was rewarding. Curiosity reinforcement introduces a state-keyed learned value `_IKG.curiosity_value[key]` that tracks the historical reward outcome of exploration in each state context. The key uses only `(int(bg_energy * 5), int(bg_pe * 10))` -- the action dimension of the Pack 1 novelty key is intentionally excluded. Including `last_action_type` in the CR key creates a pathological feedback: when the previous action was 'explore', the key has a low visit count (explore fires rarely relative to approach), producing high novelty for states recently entered via explore, which boosts explore further. Removing the action dimension de-correlates the CR key from the novelty key, preventing this self-referential cascade. Pack 4 is guarded by `enable_curiosity_reinforcement` in IkigaiContext, defaulting to False.

The curiosity_value update runs AFTER action commitment: if `_IKG.last_action_type == 'explore'`, the reward is computed as `cr_reward = (adjusted_credit_signal + 1.0) / 2.0` (normalizing ACS from [-1,1] to [0,1]), and the value is updated via EMA: `curiosity_value[key] = (1 - alpha) * old + alpha * reward`, with alpha = 0.05. All keys in curiosity_value decay by 0.999 per tick. The update check uses `last_action_type == 'explore'` (the raw BG primitive), NOT `last_action_type == 'run_experiment'` (the exec_diversity-remapped type). An initial implementation used the remapped type; since `last_action_type` stores the BG primitive before exec_diversity applies, the condition `== 'run_experiment'` was never True, curiosity_value was never updated, and all keys remained at 0 throughout the run. The fix -- changing to `== 'explore'` -- was confirmed by observing non-zero curiosity_value keys in subsequent runs.

The curiosity_value score injection runs inside the BG scoring block, after Packs 6, 1, and 3: `_sc_explore += K_CR * cr_v`; `_sc_approach -= 0.2 * K_CR * cr_v`; clamped >= 0.0. K_CR underwent three revisions due to cascade instability. At K_CR = 0.12, run_experiment rose to 91.5%: each explore selection increased curiosity_value, which boosted explore on subsequent ticks, which further increased curiosity_value. The cascade was amplified by an interaction with novelty_counts: explore states have inherently low visit counts (approach is selected much more often), so novelty score is persistently high for explore-associated state keys, providing a compounding double boost. At K_CR = 0.06 (after removing last_action_type from the CR key to break the direct self-reference), the cascade persisted at 93.1%. The mechanism shifted: each approach selection increments novelty_counts for the approach-associated state key; after approach fires for many consecutive ticks, that key saturates; when the system next switches to explore, it enters the approach-saturated state region with a different energy/PE bucket, which has a LOW count (because explore rarely visits it), HIGH novelty, and moderate curiosity_value -- producing a strong double-boost that prevents approach from recovering. Formal stability analysis: at approach equilibrium (count = count_eq), the combined novelty and curiosity_value effect must not exceed the credit gap. The stability bound is K_CR < 0.037. At K_CR = 0.03, the margin at approach equilibrium is 0.025, sufficient to allow approach to win on approach-state ticks while permitting explore to win on high-mismatch ticks. K_CR = 0.03 produces run_experiment = 9.7% at 5000 ticks with no cascading.

## 3. System Behavior (Post-Change)

With all Day 33 through Day 37 packs enabled (Day 36 Pack 1 credit_coupling excluded) over 5000 waking ticks, the exploration architecture operates as a three-layer gating system. The lowest layer (novelty, Pack 1/2) provides a background signal that transiently makes any state region with low visit history attractive to explore. Because visits accumulate and decay at rate 0.995/tick, novelty pressure is continuously renewed: approach-dominant states gradually rebuild count, reducing their own boost to approach and eventually creating a window in which a less-visited state receives a higher explore score. The second layer (contextual curiosity, Pack 3) gates this novelty signal: the multiplicative `curiosity = nov_n * mismatch` formula requires BOTH novelty AND elevated PE-credit mismatch to be non-trivial simultaneously. High-PE ticks are rare (PE mean = 0.009, PE > 0.10 on less than 2% of ticks); the coincidence of high PE and high novelty is rarer still, producing a sparse but coherent trigger for burst exploration. The third layer (curiosity reinforcement, Pack 4) applies a learned weight to state contexts in which past exploration produced positive adjusted credit, causing the system to preferentially explore in states that have historically been rewarding.

The combined effect is exploration that is bursty in time, contextually gated, and value-conditioned. Exploration is bursty because the mismatch trigger is episodic: when PE rises above baseline, explore wins several consecutive ticks until PE subsides and novelty_counts for the explore-selected state re-accumulate. Exploration is contextually gated because the curiosity_value and novelty signals are both near-zero outside their respective trigger conditions; on typical low-PE, medium-novelty ticks, the combined score injection is small enough that approach still wins the scoring block. Exploration is value-conditioned because curiosity_value is updated only on explore ticks, accumulates over time in state contexts where exploration has been rewarded, and contributes a persistent score advantage in those contexts. The organism is not randomly probing; it is preferentially probing state contexts with low visit history, high current uncertainty, and historically positive exploration outcomes -- a structure more consistent with directed curiosity than with undifferentiated noise.

top_confidence remains at its natural compressed value of approximately 0.504 throughout. The novelty and curiosity signals operate entirely within the BG scoring block and have no path to top_confidence. fallback_bg routing continues at 99.4% of waking ticks. The credit architecture (ACS, Pack 5 mean-preserving perturbation, exec_diversity) is unchanged.

## 5. Key Findings

- **Novelty direction in the score injection determines whether the signal is pro-learning or anti-learning.** The initial formulation `_sc_approach -= 0.5 * K_N * (1 - nov_n)` subtracts from approach in proportion to `(1 - nov_n)`, which is large when nov_n is small -- when the state is FAMILIAR. A mechanism that suppresses approach in familiar states effectively promotes approach in novel states, inverting the intended curiosity gradient. The correct suppression term is proportional to nov_n (suppresses approach in novel states) so that the novelty boost to explore and the suppression of approach are aligned: both favor explore when the state is unexplored. The coefficient asymmetry (1.0 to explore, 0.3 suppression of approach) is intentional: symmetric bidirectional scoring can cause oscillation between monocultures, while asymmetric injection allows exploration without fully collapsing approach.

- **Novelty without decay collapses to a constant low-amplitude bias under any realistic state space.** With 13 unique state keys and K_N = 0.12, counts saturate in under 100 ticks without decay. At count = 100, nov_n = 0.218; the novelty signal provides a 0.026 boost to explore against a 0.094 gap. This is insufficient to change the argmax outcome and is indistinguishable from a fixed offset applied uniformly to all scoring block ticks. The decay mechanism (0.995/tick) is not an optional enhancement; it is the mechanism by which novelty remains a dynamic, count-sensitive signal rather than a saturated constant. The equilibrium count under decay scales with the visit rate, producing genuinely different novelty levels for frequently-visited versus rarely-visited state contexts.

- **PE normalization is necessary for mismatch to be a dynamic signal.** Raw PE operates in a range [0.005, 0.112] under typical waking conditions; (1 - credit_n) operates in a range [0.33, 0.38] near ACS mean. The product `pe * (1 - credit_n)` is dominated by the credit term's stability: mismatch mean = 0.003, std = 0.001 -- practically constant. Dividing PE by its practical ceiling (0.10) before multiplication rescales pe_n to [0, 1] relative to the PE distribution the organism actually experiences, giving mismatch a distribution that tracks PE variance: mean = 0.032, std = 0.039, max = 0.354. The key insight is that mismatch must be a relative signal -- how elevated is PE relative to its normal operating range -- not an absolute product of two quantities with incompatible magnitudes. Normalization is signal engineering: without it, the mismatch formula is mathematically correct but functionally deaf to PE dynamics.

- **Exploration becomes bursty under contextual gating, which is the expected signature of motivated behavior.** The burst structure (mean 16.8 ticks, max 70 ticks, 27 bursts over 5000t) is not explicitly programmed: no burst count or burst length target is in the Pack 3 specification. Bursts emerge because the curiosity trigger is sparse (high PE + high novelty coincide rarely) but, when triggered, the state transitions driven by exploration keep PE elevated (explore is metabolically costly, which maintains prediction error above baseline) and keep the current state key novel (explore rarely revisits the same key on consecutive ticks). The burst self-prolongs until PE subsides or novelty_counts re-accumulate. This structure -- episodic, self-terminating, contextually triggered -- is qualitatively consistent with how biological novelty-seeking and LC-NE-driven exploration episodes are described: not random, not tonic, but phasic.

- **Cascade stability in curiosity reinforcement requires a formal bound, not empirical reduction.** Two K_CR reductions (0.12 -> 0.06 -> 0.03) were driven by distinct cascade mechanisms, not by the same problem being incompletely fixed. The K_CR = 0.12 cascade was caused by direct self-reference through novelty_counts (explore fires -> explore-associated state has low count -> high novelty boost -> more explore). Removing the action dimension from the CR key broke this mechanism. The K_CR = 0.06 cascade was driven by a cross-signal interaction: approach fires for many consecutive ticks -> approach-associated state key saturates -> when system enters explore state, those energy/PE buckets have depleted novelty_counts from approach visits -> explore-associated buckets appear novel -> double boost sustains explore. The stability bound K_CR < 0.037 is derived from margin analysis: at approach equilibrium count (count_eq ≈ 15), the combined CR + novelty boost to explore must not exceed the credit gap minus minimum required margin. K_CR = 0.03 provides margin = 0.025, confirmed by the 26/26 PASS at 5000 ticks without cascading.

- **Entropy is not a design target; it is a measurement of achieved diversity.** At Day 36 end, entropy H = 0.9440 bits was achieved through energy depletion gating (idle_recover), not through explore winning the scoring block. At Day 37 end, H = 1.0918 bits, higher than Day 36, reflecting three active action types at non-trivial rates (edit_code 73%, idle_recover 17%, run_experiment 9.7%) rather than two action types at asymmetric rates. The entropy increase is an indicator that the diversity mechanism has a different source -- scoring block competition for run_experiment, not boundary-condition routing for idle_recover alone. Had the target been entropy itself, any two-distribution split could have achieved 0.94 bits. The relevant measurement is the action-type distribution and the mechanism producing it, not the entropy value derived from it.

## 6. Failure Modes / Constraints

**Pack 1 formula inversion.** The initial suppression of approach used `(1 - nov_n)` as the weight, which is high in familiar states and low in novel states. This inverts the gradient: novel states receive minimal approach suppression (high nov_n -> low (1-nov_n) -> minimal suppression), while familiar states receive maximal approach suppression. The net effect is that the system explores less in novel states and more in familiar states -- the opposite of directed novelty-seeking. The fix is conceptually simple (substitute nov_n for (1-nov_n)) but the failure illustrates a general class of sign error in multiplicative weight schemes: when constructing a suppression term, the weight must be highest precisely when suppression is desired, not when it is tolerable.

**Pack 1 saturation without decay.** With 13 state keys and no count decay, all keys accumulate counts in proportion to their visit rate. Any key visited more than ~200 times -- achievable in under 100 ticks at the approach-dominant visit rate -- saturates to nov_n < 0.22. Below this value, K_N = 0.12 provides a boost of 0.026, insufficient to close a gap of 0.094. The saturation is not a low-probability edge case: it occurs on every run of sufficient length. Without decay, novelty pressure functions for approximately 100 ticks before becoming inert. The decay constant 0.995/tick (half-life approximately 138 ticks) was chosen to sustain the novelty signal indefinitely at equilibrium counts compatible with non-trivial nov_n values.

**Pack 3 mismatch formula, first two iterations.** Iteration 1 (abs difference from expected) produces a constant bias that is structurally incapable of providing contextual discrimination: mismatch is dominated by the (1 - credit_n) term, which varies only across the credit variance range (std = 0.02), not across PE dynamics. Iteration 2 (raw product pe * (1 - credit_n)) is contextually correct in form but dormant in practice because PE magnitude is three orders of magnitude smaller than credit_n magnitude. Both failures are versions of the same structural error: applying a formula correct in intent to signals that do not share a common scale. The normalization fix (pe / 0.10) maps PE into the same practical range as credit_n, enabling the product to carry joint information about both signals. The harness metrics code must be updated simultaneously with the ikigai.py injection when the formula changes; running the harness with the old display formula after updating ikigai.py produced misleading diagnostic output during iteration 2 (showing mismatch values that reflected the old formula while the system ran the new one).

**Pack 4 action type key error.** `_IKG.last_action_type` stores the BG primitive label ('explore', 'approach', 'wait', 'idle_recover') set from `bound_action['action_type']` at the point of BG selection, BEFORE exec_diversity remaps 'explore' to 'run_experiment'. The initial curiosity_value update checked `if last_action_type == 'run_experiment'`, which references the exec_diversity output, not the BG primitive. This condition was never True: last_action_type never holds the exec_diversity-remapped type. The curiosity_value dictionary remained empty for the entire run; K_CR contributed nothing to scoring. The error is invisible from behavioral metrics alone (explore still fires at Pack 3 rates without CR contribution) and only detectable by inspecting curiosity_value key counts, which remained at 0. The fix is to check `== 'explore'` consistently with the variable's actual content.

**Pack 4 cascades at K_CR = 0.12 and K_CR = 0.06.** Detailed in the Key Findings section. The two cascades have different mechanisms (direct self-reference via novelty_counts vs cross-signal interaction via approach-count depletion), which is why fixing one did not prevent the other. The cascade at K_CR = 0.06 is subtler: it requires the system to be in a specific state (approach firing long enough to deplete the approach-associated novelty_counts) before the explore-associated buckets gain a compound advantage. Empirically detecting this cascade required runs of at least 2000 ticks; at 500 ticks the cascade had not yet fully developed and the run appeared stable. The stability bound derivation (K_CR < 0.037) provides a condition that holds regardless of which cascade pathway is active.

## 7. Architectural Insights

**Decay as the equilibrium mechanism for count-based signals.** Any signal derived from a running count accumulates to saturation in the absence of a decay mechanism, given sufficient time. The novelty_counts case demonstrates this at the granularity of a few hundred ticks. The decay constant 0.995/tick sets the equilibrium count at count_eq = visit_rate / (1 - 0.995), making the equilibrium proportional to how often the state is visited. Frequently-visited states equilibrate at higher counts (lower nov_n) and rarely-visited states equilibrate at lower counts (higher nov_n) -- a self-calibrating property that requires no explicit normalization step. The decay mechanism is not specific to novelty; any learned or accumulated signal in the BG scoring block that could saturate should include a comparable decay term. curiosity_value uses the same principle: decay 0.999/tick means the value is sustained only as long as the state is repeatedly rewarding; it fades when the organism stops exploring that context.

**Burst structure is a diagnostic signature of episodic gating, not a design target.** The 16.8-tick mean burst length is not a consequence of any parameter controlling burst length. It is determined by the interaction of PE duration (how long a PE excursion lasts), novelty_counts re-accumulation rate (how quickly the explored state key becomes familiar again), and the credit gap (how large an explore lead is required to sustain explore selection across consecutive ticks). Because these are all continuous signals with their own dynamics, the burst length distribution is emergent. The presence of a burst structure -- rather than uniformly distributed explore events -- is the observable signature that the exploration mechanism is contextually gated: uniform independent probability of explore would produce Poisson-distributed inter-event intervals, not the clustered intervals observed. The burst statistics in V5b are therefore not just performance metrics; they are structural evidence that Pack 3 is implementing contextual gating rather than a stochastic override.

**Cascade stability analysis must be performed before deploying learned-value signals into competitive scoring blocks.** Reinforcement learning in a multi-action scoring block without regularization is susceptible to winner-take-all dynamics: any action that wins increases its associated value (or reduces the value of competing states), which makes it more likely to win again. The standard result in multi-armed bandit theory is that without sufficient exploration pressure or regularization, value learning degenerates to monoculture. K_CR < 0.037 is the bound at which the learned curiosity_value can boost explore on appropriate ticks without creating an approach-to-explore conversion so strong that approach cannot recover. The bound exists because the score gap between approach and explore (0.094 at ACS mean) sets a ceiling on the permissible reinforcement strength. Any system that introduces a learned value signal into a threshold-bounded scoring block must verify that the maximum achievable value (here, curiosity_value max = 0.233, K_CR = 0.03, contribution = 0.007) is bounded relative to the natural score gaps that maintain behavioral diversity.

**Exploration transitioned from boundary-condition-driven to scoring-block-driven between Day 36 and Day 37.** In Day 36, idle_recover diversity (36%) emerged from the energy depletion gate (bg_energy < BG_ENERGY_LOW = 0.30) bypassing the scoring block entirely. run_experiment diversity was zero because explore never won the scoring block. In Day 37, run_experiment diversity (9.7%) is produced by explore winning the scoring block on a subset of ticks -- specifically, ticks where the combined novelty, contextual curiosity, and reinforcement injection exceeds the credit gap. This is a qualitatively different mechanism: the diversity is signal-driven and policy-responsive rather than boundary-driven and state-threshold-responsive. The significance is that signal-driven diversity can adapt to changes in the credit landscape (as ACS varies, the gap changes, and the novelty threshold adjusts accordingly), whereas boundary-driven diversity is fixed by BG_ENERGY_LOW and energy dynamics regardless of credit state.

## 9. Next Direction (NOT IMPLEMENTED)

The exploration architecture is now functional: run_experiment at 9.7% is within the target range, the mechanism is policy-driven, and temporal structure (bursting) is consistent with biological curiosity models. The next architectural gap is reward attribution at the exploration outcome level. Currently, curiosity_value is updated based on adjusted_credit_signal at the moment the explore action is committed -- this is the credit signal available at selection time, not the outcome signal measured after the explore action has been executed. The OutcomeEvaluator's actual_reward is computed from energy_delta, goal_progress, and stability over the subsequent tick, but this signal is not separately tracked for explore-type ticks. A delayed credit attribution mechanism -- one that updates curiosity_value not at explore selection but one or more ticks after, reading the actual_reward produced by the run_experiment execution -- would provide a more causally accurate reward signal for the curiosity memory system. The implementation would require storing the active curiosity_value key at explore selection time and applying the EMA update on the next tick conditioned on the previous tick's explore flag.

A second direction is the relationship between the novelty key and the curiosity_value key. Currently, novelty uses a three-tuple key `(last_action_type, energy_bucket, pe_bucket)` while curiosity_value uses a two-tuple key `(energy_bucket, pe_bucket)`. The action dimension of the novelty key provides finer state discrimination but also creates 13 keys in the current setup (5 energy buckets * 10 PE buckets compressed to active ranges, times 3 action types observed). Curiosity_value's simpler key (6 active keys at 5000t) generalizes across action types. Whether this generalization is appropriate depends on whether the reward from exploring in a given energy-PE regime is consistent regardless of which action immediately preceded it. If approach-preceded explore and wait-preceded explore have systematically different outcomes (because approach vs wait leaves the system in different energy and PE states at the moment explore fires), the two-tuple key would conflate distinct contexts. Empirical validation of curiosity_value variance by preceding action type would determine whether the key should be extended.
